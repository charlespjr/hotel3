Your Complete Guide to Hotels in Jacksonville, FL

Jacksonville, Florida's largest city by area, offers visitors a diverse range of hotel options across its many neighborhoods. Whether you're visiting for business, pleasure, or a little of both, this guide will help you navigate Jacksonville's hotel landscape.

1. Popular Hotel Districts and Neighborhoods

Jacksonville's hotels are concentrated in several key areas:

  • Downtown Jacksonville: The city's business hub with upscale hotels and convention centers
  • Southbank: Across the river from downtown, offering great views and riverfront access
  • Jacksonville Beach: Coastal hotels with ocean views and beach access
  • St. Johns Town Center: Upscale shopping district with many hotel options
  • Airport Area (near JAX): Convenient for business travelers and early flights
  • Baymeadows: Mid-range hotels near major highways and business parks

2. Average Hotel Prices and Seasonal Variations

Jacksonville hotel prices vary significantly by season and location:

  • Budget hotels: $70-$120 per night
  • Mid-range hotels: $120-$200 per night
  • Luxury hotels: $200-$400+ per night
  • Beachfront properties: Prices increase 20-40% during peak season

Seasonal variations: Prices are highest from March-May (spring break and warmer weather) and October-November (pleasant fall weather). Summer months can be hot but offer better deals, especially at beach properties.

3. Types of Hotels Available

Jacksonville offers accommodation for every taste and budget:

  • Luxury: The Ritz-Carlton, Amelia Island; Omni Jacksonville; Marriott Jacksonville Downtown
  • Boutique: The Lodge & Club at Ponte Vedra Beach; Casa Marina Hotel
  • Budget: Red Roof Inn, Motel 6, Microtel Inn
  • Extended Stay: Residence Inn, Homewood Suites, Candlewood Suites
  • Beach Resorts: One Ocean Resort & Spa; Hampton Inn Jacksonville Beach
  • Business Hotels: Hyatt Regency Jacksonville Riverfront; DoubleTree by Hilton

4. Nearby Attractions and Points of Interest

Jacksonville offers numerous attractions near major hotel districts:

  • Downtown/Southbank: Jacksonville Landing, Museum of Science & History, Friendship Fountain
  • Beach Areas: Jacksonville Beach Pier, Kathryn Abbey Hanna Park
  • Cultural: Cummer Museum of Art & Gardens, Jacksonville Zoo and Gardens
  • Sports: TIAA Bank Field (Jacksonville Jaguars), VyStar Veterans Memorial Arena
  • Nature: Timucuan Ecological and Historic Preserve, Little Talbot Island State Park
  • Golf: TPC Sawgrass (home of THE PLAYERS Championship)

5. Transportation Options and Accessibility

Getting around Jacksonville:

  • Public Transit: JTA buses and Skyway monorail (limited coverage)
  • Rental Cars: Available at airport and throughout city (recommended for most visitors)
  • Ride Shares: Uber and Lyft widely available
  • Taxis: Available but less convenient than ride shares
  • Airport: Jacksonville International Airport (JAX) with many hotel shuttles
  • Accessibility: Most hotels comply with ADA requirements; confirm specific needs when booking

6. Best Times to Visit

Ideal times to visit Jacksonville depend on your preferences:

  • Weather: March-May and October-November offer the most pleasant temperatures
  • Events: Consider THE PLAYERS Championship (March), Jacksonville Jazz Festival (May), or Florida-Georgia football game (October)
  • Budget Travel: Summer months (June-August) offer lower rates despite warmer weather
  • Beach Visits: Late spring and early fall offer warm water without peak crowds

7. Special Considerations for Travelers

Important factors to consider when booking:

  • Business Travelers: Downtown hotels offer best access to business districts and convention centers
  • Families: Look for hotels with pools and easy beach access; many offer free stays for kids
  • Pet Owners: Many Jacksonville hotels are pet-friendly (often with fees)
  • Accessibility Needs: Confirm specific accommodations with hotels in advance
  • Extended Stays: Consider hotels with kitchenettes or nearby grocery options

Pro Tip:

If visiting for a beach vacation, consider staying in Ponte Vedra Beach or Amelia Island for a more upscale experience, or Jacksonville Beach for more budget-friendly options.

8. Tips for Finding the Best Deals

Ways to save on Jacksonville hotels:

  • Book directly with hotels for potential discounts or perks
  • Check for package deals combining hotel with rental car or flights
  • Look for last-minute deals on apps like HotelTonight
  • Consider staying slightly inland for better rates than beachfront properties
  • Join hotel loyalty programs for potential upgrades or discounts
  • Travel during shoulder seasons (late spring or early fall) for best rates
  • Check for corporate or government rates if applicable

9. Local Amenities and Services

What to expect from Jacksonville hotels:

  • Dining: Most hotels offer breakfast; many have restaurants or room service
  • Fitness: Most mid-range and up hotels have fitness centers
  • Business Centers: Common in business-oriented hotels
  • WiFi: Nearly universal, though some charge for premium speeds
  • Parking: Often available but may incur daily fees at downtown hotels
  • Concierge: Available at higher-end
