Your Complete Guide to Hotels in Jacksonville, FL

Jacksonville, Florida's largest city by area, offers visitors beautiful beaches, vibrant culture, and diverse accommodations. Whether you're visiting for business or pleasure, this guide will help you navigate Jacksonville's hotel scene.

1. Popular Hotel Districts and Neighborhoods

Jacksonville's hotels are concentrated in several key areas:

  • Downtown Jacksonville: Business hotels near the convention center and sports venues
  • Jacksonville Beach: Oceanfront resorts and family-friendly hotels
  • Southside: Convenient location near airport and business parks
  • St. Johns Town Center: Upscale hotels near premier shopping
  • Amelia Island: Luxury resorts and historic inns (north of Jacksonville)
  • Riverside/Avondale: Boutique hotels in historic neighborhoods

2. Average Hotel Prices and Seasonal Variations

Jacksonville hotel prices fluctuate significantly by season:

  • Budget hotels: $70-$120/night (off-season), $100-$180/night (peak)
  • Mid-range hotels: $120-$200/night (off-season), $180-$300/night (peak)
  • Luxury hotels/resorts: $250-$500+/night year-round

Peak seasons: March-April (spring break), June-August (summer), December-January (holidays)

Best value: September-November and February (excluding holidays)

3. Types of Hotels Available

Jacksonville offers accommodations for every taste and budget:

  • Luxury: The Ritz-Carlton Amelia Island, One Ocean Resort & Spa
  • Boutique: The Lodge & Club at Ponte Vedra Beach, Hotel Indigo Jacksonville-Deerwood Park
  • Budget: Red Roof Inn, Motel 6, various chain hotels
  • Extended Stay: Residence Inn, Homewood Suites, Candlewood Suites
  • Beach Resorts: Margaritaville at Jacksonville Beach, Casa Marina Hotel
  • Business Hotels: Hyatt Regency Jacksonville Riverfront, DoubleTree by Hilton

4. Nearby Attractions and Points of Interest

Top attractions near Jacksonville hotels:

  • Beaches: Jacksonville Beach, Neptune Beach, Atlantic Beach
  • Cultural: Cummer Museum of Art & Gardens, Museum of Science & History (MOSH)
  • Outdoors: Kathryn Abbey Hanna Park, Little Talbot Island State Park
  • Sports: TIAA Bank Field (NFL Jaguars), VyStar Veterans Memorial Arena
  • Family: Jacksonville Zoo and Gardens, Adventure Landing water park
  • Dining/Nightlife: Jacksonville Landing, Five Points in Riverside

5. Transportation Options and Accessibility

Getting around Jacksonville:

  • Airport: Jacksonville International Airport (JAX) - 15 miles north of downtown
  • Public Transit: JTA buses and Skyway monorail (limited coverage)
  • Rental Cars: Available at airport and throughout city (recommended)
  • Ride Shares: Uber and Lyft widely available
  • Taxis: Available but less common than ride shares
  • Accessibility: Most hotels comply with ADA requirements; confirm specific needs when booking

6. Best Times to Visit

Consider these factors when planning your trip:

  • Weather: Best in spring (March-May) and fall (September-November)
  • Events: Florida-Georgia football game (October), Jacksonville Jazz Festival (May)
  • Beach Season: Water warm enough for swimming May-October
  • Hurricane Season: June-November (peak August-October)
  • Business Travel: Year-round, with conventions spread throughout

7. Special Considerations for Travelers

  • Families: Look for hotels with pools and kid-friendly amenities
  • Business Travelers: Downtown and Southside offer best proximity to offices
  • Disabled Travelers: Confirm ADA compliance and accessibility features
  • Pet Owners: Many hotels are pet-friendly (often with fees)
  • International Visitors: Major hotels have staff fluent in multiple languages

8. Tips for Finding the Best Deals

  • Book early for major events (football games, concerts)
  • Check hotel websites for exclusive promotions
  • Consider package deals with flights and rental cars
  • Join hotel loyalty programs for member rates
  • Look for last-minute deals on apps like HotelTonight
  • Travel mid-week for lower rates at business hotels
  • Visit during shoulder seasons (February, September-November)

9. Local Amenities and Services

What to expect from Jacksonville hotels:

  • Dining: Most hotels have restaurants; many offer free breakfast
  • WiFi: Typically free, though some luxury hotels charge
  • Parking: Often available but may have fees (especially downtown)
  • Business Centers: Common in business-oriented hotels
  • Fitness Centers: Standard in mid-range and upscale hotels
  • Concierge: Available at higher-end properties

10. Safety and Security Information

  • Most tourist areas and hotels are safe, but practice normal precautions
  • Downtown can be quiet at night outside event areas
  • Beach areas are generally safe but watch for strong currents
  • Hotels have security measures - ask about them when booking
  • Use hotel safes for valuables
  • Be cautious when walking alone at night in less populated areas
  • Emergency number: 911

Final Thoughts

Jacksonville offers diverse hotel options to suit every traveler's needs. Whether you want a beachfront resort, downtown business hotel, or budget-friendly accommodation, you'll find it in Florida's largest city.
