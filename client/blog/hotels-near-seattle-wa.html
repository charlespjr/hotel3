Your Complete Guide to Hotels in Seattle, Washington

Seattle, the Emerald City, offers a diverse range of accommodations to suit every traveler's needs. From luxury high-rises with Space Needle views to cozy boutique hotels in historic neighborhoods, this guide will help you navigate Seattle's hotel landscape.

1. Popular Hotel Districts and Neighborhoods

Downtown Seattle

The heart of the city with the highest concentration of hotels, close to major attractions and business centers.

Belltown

Trendy neighborhood with boutique hotels, nightlife, and easy access to the waterfront.

South Lake Union

Tech hub with modern hotels, near the Museum of History & Industry (MOHAI) and Lake Union.

Pioneer Square

Historic district with charming hotels, art galleries, and proximity to stadiums.

Queen Anne

Residential area with B&Bs and smaller hotels, offering great views and a quieter atmosphere.

Capitol Hill

Vibrant, artsy neighborhood with unique boutique accommodations and excellent dining options.

2. Average Hotel Prices and Seasonal Variations

Seattle hotel prices fluctuate significantly based on season and events:

  • Budget hotels: $100-$200 per night
  • Mid-range hotels: $200-$350 per night
  • Luxury hotels: $350-$600+ per night

Seasonal Variations:

  • Peak season (June-August): Highest prices, book 3-6 months in advance
  • Shoulder seasons (April-May, September-October): Moderate prices, pleasant weather
  • Low season (November-March): Best deals, except around holidays
Pro Tip: Hotel prices spike during major events like PAX West (August/September), Seafair (July-August), and major conventions at the Washington State Convention Center.

4. Nearby Attractions and Points of Interest

Most hotels are conveniently located near major Seattle attractions:

  • Space Needle & Seattle Center: 5-15 minutes from downtown hotels
  • Pike Place Market: Walkable from most downtown hotels
  • Museum of Pop Culture (MoPOP): At Seattle Center
  • Chihuly Garden and Glass: Next to Space Needle
  • Waterfront: Great Wheel, Seattle Aquarium, ferry terminal
  • Stadiums: T-Mobile Park (Mariners) and Lumen Field (Seahawks)

5. Transportation Options and Accessibility

From Seattle-Tacoma International Airport (SEA):

  • Light Rail: $3.25, about 40 minutes to downtown
  • Ride shares: $35-$50 to downtown
  • Taxis: Flat rate $40 to downtown
  • Hotel shuttles: Some hotels offer free or paid shuttles

Getting Around Seattle:

  • Walking: Downtown is very walkable
  • Public Transit: Extensive bus system and light rail
  • Streetcar: Connects South Lake Union to Capitol Hill
  • Bikes/Scooters: Numerous rental options
  • Ferries: To Bainbridge Island and beyond
Accessibility Note: Most major hotels comply with ADA requirements, but older boutique hotels may have limited accessibility. Always confirm specific needs when booking.

6. Best Times to Visit

  • Summer (June-August): Best weather but most crowded
  • Spring (April-May): Fewer crowds, blooming flowers
  • Fall (September-October): Pleasant temperatures, fall colors
  • Winter (November-March): Lowest prices but rainy

For festival lovers, consider timing your visit with events like the Northwest Folklife Festival (May) or Bumbershoot (September).

7. Special Considerations for Travelers

Business Travelers

  • Choose downtown or South Lake Union for proximity to business centers
  • Look for hotels with business centers and meeting spaces
  • Consider proximity to Washington State Convention Center

Families

  • Look for hotels with pools and family suites
  • Consider proximity to Seattle Center (Children's Museum, Pacific Science Center)
  • Check for extra bed policies

Pet Owners

  • Many hotels are pet-friendly (often with fees)
  • Nearby dog parks: Denny Park, Magnusson Park

8. Tips for Finding the Best Deals

  • Book directly with hotels for potential perks or price matching
  • Sign up for hotel loyalty programs
  • Check for package deals (hotel + attraction tickets)
  • Consider last-minute booking apps for same-day deals
  • Look beyond downtown for better values (University District, Ballard)
