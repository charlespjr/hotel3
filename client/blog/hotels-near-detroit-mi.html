Your Complete Guide to Hotels in Detroit, Michigan

Detroit, the Motor City, has undergone a remarkable renaissance in recent years, making it an increasingly popular destination for business and leisure travelers alike. This guide will help you navigate the city's hotel options, neighborhoods, and everything you need to know for a comfortable stay.

1. Popular Hotel Districts and Neighborhoods

Detroit offers several distinct areas with hotel accommodations, each with its own character and advantages:

  • Downtown Detroit - The heart of the city with the highest concentration of hotels, near major attractions and business centers.
  • Corktown - Detroit's oldest neighborhood with boutique hotels and a hip, historic vibe.
  • Midtown - Cultural hub near museums and universities, offering a mix of boutique and chain hotels.
  • New Center - Business district with convention-friendly hotels and historic architecture.
  • Greektown - Entertainment district with casino hotels and vibrant nightlife.
  • Riverfront - Scenic area with luxury hotels offering views of the Detroit River and Canada.

2. Average Hotel Prices and Seasonal Variations

Hotel prices in Detroit vary significantly by season, location, and type of accommodation:

  • Budget hotels: $70-$120 per night
  • Mid-range hotels: $120-$250 per night
  • Luxury hotels: $250-$500+ per night
  • Boutique hotels: $150-$350 per night

Seasonal variations:

  • Peak season: May-September (highest prices, especially during major events)
  • Shoulder season: April and October (moderate prices, pleasant weather)
  • Off-season: November-March (lowest prices, except around holidays)

Pro Tip: Hotel prices can spike dramatically during major events like the North American International Auto Show (June), Movement Electronic Music Festival (May), or Detroit Jazz Festival (September). Book well in advance for these periods.

4. Nearby Attractions and Points of Interest

Detroit offers numerous attractions near major hotel districts:

  • Downtown: Campus Martius Park, Guardian Building, Fox Theatre, Comerica Park, Little Caesars Arena
  • Riverfront: Detroit RiverWalk, Belle Isle Park, GM Renaissance Center
  • Cultural: Detroit Institute of Arts, Motown Museum, Charles H. Wright Museum of African American History
  • Historical: Ford Piquette Avenue Plant, Michigan Central Station (under renovation), Fort Wayne
  • Family-friendly: Detroit Zoo, Michigan Science Center, LEGOLAND Discovery Center

5. Transportation Options and Accessibility

Getting around Detroit from your hotel:

  • People Mover: Elevated automated transit system circling downtown (free until 2024, then $0.75)
  • QLine: Streetcar running along Woodward Avenue from downtown to New Center ($1.50 fare)
  • Bus: DDOT and SMART bus systems serve the metro area ($2 fare)
  • Ride-sharing: Uber and Lyft widely available
  • Bike share: MoGo bike share system with stations throughout the city
  • Airport transportation: Detroit Metro Airport (DTW) is about 20 miles from downtown; taxis, shuttles, and ride-sharing available

Accessibility Note: Most major hotels and downtown attractions are wheelchair accessible, but some historic buildings may have limitations. Always check with your hotel about specific accessibility needs.

6. Best Times to Visit

The ideal time to visit Detroit depends on your priorities:

  • For pleasant weather: Late May through early October
  • For budget travelers: January-March (excluding holiday periods)
  • For festivals and events: Summer months (especially June-August)
  • For sports fans: April-October (baseball), October-April (hockey/basketball)
  • For holiday atmosphere: December (Campus Martius ice skating, holiday markets)

7. Special Considerations for Travelers

  • Business travelers: Downtown hotels offer best access to business districts and convention centers
  • Families: Look for hotels near the RiverWalk or with pools; some offer family packages
  • Pet owners: Several pet-friendly hotels available, typically with weight limits and fees
