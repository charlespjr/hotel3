Hotels in Detroit, MI: A Complete Travel Guide

Detroit, Michigan, is a city rich in history, culture, and revitalization. Whether you're visiting for business, leisure, or a special event, choosing the right hotel can make all the difference. This guide covers everything you need to know about hotels in Detroit, from neighborhoods and pricing to attractions and safety tips.

1. Popular Hotel Districts and Neighborhoods

Detroit has several key areas where hotels are concentrated, each offering a unique experience:

  • Downtown Detroit – The heart of the city, home to luxury hotels, casinos, and major attractions like the Renaissance Center.
  • Corktown – One of Detroit’s oldest neighborhoods, known for its historic charm and trendy dining scene.
  • Midtown – A cultural hub with museums, theaters, and proximity to Wayne State University.
  • New Center – A business-friendly district with boutique hotels and easy access to the QLine streetcar.
  • Greektown – A lively area with casinos, nightlife, and Greek-inspired restaurants.

2. Average Hotel Prices and Seasonal Variations

Hotel prices in Detroit vary based on location, season, and demand:

  • Budget hotels: $70–$120 per night
  • Mid-range hotels: $120–$250 per night
  • Luxury hotels: $250–$500+ per night

Peak seasons (higher prices) include summer (June–August) and major events like the North American International Auto Show. Winter months (January–March) often offer lower rates.

3. Types of Hotels Available

Detroit offers a variety of accommodations to suit different preferences:

  • Luxury Hotels: The Shinola Hotel, Detroit Foundation Hotel, and The Westin Book Cadillac
  • Budget Hotels: Red Roof Inn, Motel 6, and Holiday Inn Express
  • Boutique Hotels: Siren Hotel, Element Detroit at the Metropolitan
  • Extended Stay: Residence Inn by Marriott, Homewood Suites
  • Casino Hotels: MGM Grand Detroit, MotorCity Casino Hotel

4. Nearby Attractions and Points of Interest

Detroit has plenty to see and do near major hotels:

  • Downtown: GM Renaissance Center, Detroit Riverwalk, Campus Martius Park
  • Cultural Sites: Detroit Institute of Arts, Motown Museum, Charles H. Wright Museum
  • Sports & Entertainment: Comerica Park (Tigers), Ford Field (Lions), Little Caesars Arena (Red Wings, Pistons)
  • Dining & Nightlife: Greektown, Eastern Market, Corktown’s food scene

5. Transportation Options and Accessibility

Getting around Detroit is easy with these options:

  • Public Transit: QLine streetcar, DDOT buses, and the People Mover (downtown loop)
  • Ride-Sharing: Uber and Lyft are widely available
  • Biking: MoGo bike-share program
  • Airport: Detroit Metropolitan Airport (DTW) is about 20 miles from downtown

6. Best Times to Visit

The ideal time to visit depends on your preferences:

  • Spring (April–May): Mild weather, fewer crowds
  • Summer (June–August): Festivals, outdoor activities, but higher prices
  • Fall (September–October): Pleasant weather, fall foliage
  • Winter (November–March): Lower hotel rates, but cold temperatures

7. Special Considerations for Travelers

  • Business Travelers: Downtown hotels offer conference spaces and easy access to the Cobo Center.
  • Families: Look for hotels near the Detroit Zoo or with kid-friendly amenities.
  • Pet Owners: Some hotels, like Aloft Detroit, are pet-friendly.

8. Tips for Finding the Best Deals

  • Book in advance for major events or holidays.
  • Check hotel websites for exclusive discounts.
  • Use price comparison tools like Kayak or Booking.com.
  • Consider bundled packages (hotel + flight deals).

9. Local Amenities and Services

Most hotels offer:

  • Free Wi-Fi
  • On-site dining or nearby restaurants
  • Fitness centers and pools (luxury hotels)
  • 24-hour front desk service

10. Safety and Security Information

Detroit has improved safety in recent years, but it’s still wise to:

  • Stay in well-lit, populated areas at night.
  • Use hotel safes for valuables.
  • Research neighborhoods before booking.
  • Opt for hotels with 24/7 security.

Final Thoughts

Detroit offers a diverse range of hotels to fit every budget and travel style. Whether you're looking for luxury in downtown or a cozy boutique stay in Midtown, planning ahead will ensure a smooth and enjoyable trip.
