Hotels in Sacramento, CA: A Complete Travel Guide

Sacramento, California's capital city, offers a mix of historic charm, modern attractions, and a thriving food scene. Whether you're visiting for business, leisure, or government-related travel, choosing the right hotel can enhance your experience. This guide covers everything you need to know about hotels in Sacramento.

1. Popular Hotel Districts and Neighborhoods

Sacramento has several distinct areas where hotels are concentrated, each with its own character:

  • Downtown/Midtown: The heart of Sacramento with government buildings, entertainment venues, and upscale hotels.
  • Old Sacramento: Historic district with boutique hotels near the waterfront and tourist attractions.
  • Natomas: Near Sacramento International Airport with many chain hotels offering airport shuttles.
  • Arden-Arcade: Suburban area with mid-range hotels near shopping centers and Cal Expo.
  • West Sacramento: Across the river from downtown, offering more affordable options with easy access to the city.

2. Average Hotel Prices and Seasonal Variations

Hotel prices in Sacramento vary by season and location:

  • Budget hotels: $80-$120 per night
  • Mid-range hotels: $120-$200 per night
  • Luxury hotels: $200-$400+ per night

Seasonal variations:

  • Peak seasons: Spring (March-May) and Fall (September-October) when weather is ideal
  • Higher prices during major events like the California State Fair (July) and legislative sessions
  • Lower prices in winter (except around holidays) and summer (when temperatures soar)

3. Types of Hotels Available

Sacramento offers accommodation options for every preference and budget:

  • Luxury: The Citizen Hotel, Kimpton Sawyer Hotel, Hyatt Regency Sacramento
  • Boutique: The Sterling Hotel, Amber House Bed and Breakfast
  • Budget: Motel 6, Super 8, Econo Lodge
  • Business hotels: Sheraton Grand Sacramento, Hilton Sacramento Arden West
  • Extended stay: Residence Inn, Homewood Suites, Extended Stay America
  • Historic: Delta King Hotel (a permanently moored riverboat)

4. Nearby Attractions and Points of Interest

Many hotels are conveniently located near Sacramento's top attractions:

  • California State Capitol Museum
  • Old Sacramento State Historic Park
  • Crocker Art Museum
  • Sutter's Fort State Historic Park
  • Sacramento Zoo
  • Golden 1 Center (home of the Sacramento Kings)
  • American River Parkway (for biking and outdoor activities)
  • Farmers markets (especially the Sunday market under the freeway)

5. Transportation Options and Accessibility

Getting around Sacramento from your hotel:

  • Sacramento International Airport (SMF): 10 miles northwest of downtown with many hotels offering shuttles
  • Public transit: SacRT buses and light rail serve most areas
  • Bike share: JUMP bikes available throughout the city
  • Ride-sharing: Uber and Lyft widely available
  • Car rentals: Available at the airport and throughout the city
  • Amtrak: Sacramento Valley Station connects to Bay Area and beyond

Pro Tip: Downtown hotels are walkable to many attractions, while suburban hotels may require a car or rideshare for getting around.

6. Best Times to Visit

The ideal time to visit Sacramento depends on your preferences:

  • Spring (March-May): Mild temperatures (60s-70s°F), blooming gardens, ideal for outdoor activities
  • Fall (September-November): Pleasant weather, harvest festivals, fewer crowds
  • Winter (December-February): Cool and rainy (50s°F), lowest hotel rates (except holidays)
  • Summer (June-August): Hot (often 90s-100s°F), good for early morning/evening activities, some hotel deals

7. Special Considerations for Travelers

Keep these factors in mind when booking your Sacramento hotel:

  • Government travelers: Many downtown hotels cater to state government visitors with business amenities
  • Families: Look for hotels with pools and suites; suburban hotels often have more space
  • Accessibility: Most major hotels comply with ADA requirements; confirm specific needs when booking
  • Pet owners: Many hotels are pet-friendly, especially in the Kimpton chain
  • Convention attendees: The SAFE Credit Union Convention Center is downtown

8. Tips for Finding the Best Deals

Strategies to save on Sacramento hotels:

  1. Book midweek when business travelers leave (weekends can be cheaper downtown)
  2. Check for government or corporate rates if eligible
  3. Look for packages that include parking (downtown parking can be expensive)
  4. Consider last-minute apps like HotelTonight for same-day deals
  5. Join hotel loyalty programs for potential upgrades and perks
  6. Visit during slower periods (winter, except holidays; summer when it's hottest)
  7. Check for special event rates during conferences or conventions

9. Local Amenities and Services

What to expect from Sacramento hotels and surrounding areas:

  • Dining: Many hotels have restaurants; downtown/midtown offers excellent farm-to-fork options
  • Grocery stores: Safeway, Raley's, and Nugget Markets near most hotel areas
  • Gyms: Most hotels have fitness centers; some offer passes to local gyms
  • Business centers: Available at most business-oriented hotels
