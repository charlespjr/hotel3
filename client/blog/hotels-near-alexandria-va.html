The Ultimate Guide to Hotels in Alexandria, Virginia

Alexandria, Virginia, with its charming Old Town, waterfront views, and proximity to Washington D.C., offers a variety of hotel options for every type of traveler. Whether you're visiting for business, pleasure, or a bit of both, this guide will help you navigate Alexandria's hotel scene.

1. Popular Hotel Districts and Neighborhoods

Alexandria's hotels are concentrated in several key areas, each with its own character:

  • Old Town Alexandria - The historic heart of the city with cobblestone streets, boutique shops, and waterfront dining. Hotels here tend to be upscale or boutique.
  • Carlyle District - A business-oriented area near the federal courthouse with modern hotels convenient for government travelers.
  • Landmark/Van Dorn - More affordable options located slightly west of Old Town, near I-395.
  • Potomac Yard - A developing area with newer hotels, close to the Potomac Yard shopping center and future Metro station.
  • King Street Metro Area - Convenient for those needing access to the Metro's Blue and Yellow lines.

2. Average Hotel Prices and Seasonal Variations

Hotel prices in Alexandria vary significantly by season and location:

  • Budget hotels: $90-$150 per night
  • Mid-range hotels: $150-$250 per night
  • Luxury hotels: $250-$400+ per night

Seasonal variations:

  • Peak seasons: Spring (especially during cherry blossom season) and fall see the highest prices.
  • Shoulder seasons: Summer can be slightly less expensive, though weekends remain busy.
  • Best deals: Winter (excluding holiday periods) typically offers the lowest rates.

3. Types of Hotels Available

Alexandria offers diverse lodging options:

  • Luxury Hotels: The Alexandrian, Autograph Collection; Lorien Hotel & Spa
  • Boutique Hotels: Morrison House, Hotel Heron
  • Business Hotels: Westin Alexandria, Hilton Alexandria Old Town
  • Budget Options: Comfort Inn Alexandria, Red Roof Inn Alexandria
  • Extended Stay: Residence Inn Alexandria, Homewood Suites by Hilton
  • Historic Inns: The Alexandrian (with modern amenities in a historic setting)

4. Nearby Attractions and Points of Interest

Alexandria's hotels put you close to many attractions:

  • Old Town Waterfront - Dining, shopping, and scenic Potomac River views
  • Torpedo Factory Art Center - Working artist studios in a historic building
  • George Washington's Mount Vernon - Just 8 miles south of Alexandria
  • King Street Mile - Historic shopping and dining district
  • National Harbor - Across the river in Maryland, featuring the Capital Wheel
  • Easy access to Washington D.C. - Just minutes away via Metro or car

5. Transportation Options and Accessibility

Getting around Alexandria is convenient:

  • Metro: Blue and Yellow lines serve Alexandria with stations at King Street, Braddock Road, Eisenhower Avenue, and Van Dorn Street.
  • Free King Street Trolley: Runs from King Street Metro to the waterfront.
  • Biking: Bike share stations and the Mount Vernon Trail provide scenic routes.
  • Airports: Reagan National Airport (DCA) is just 4 miles away; Dulles (IAD) is about 30 miles west.
  • Parking: Many hotels charge for parking ($20-$40/night); some Old Town hotels have limited or no parking.

6. Best Times to Visit

The ideal time to visit Alexandria depends on your preferences:

  • For pleasant weather: April-May and September-October offer mild temperatures.
  • For festivals: Summer features numerous events but can be hot and humid.
  • For fewer crowds: January-February (excluding holiday weekends).
  • For cherry blossoms: Late March to early April (but book early as hotels fill quickly).

7. Special Considerations for Travelers

  • Business travelers: Carlyle District hotels offer proximity to government offices.
  • Families: Look for hotels with pools and suites; Old Town is very walkable with kids.
  • Pet owners: Many Alexandria hotels are pet-friendly, often with fees.
  • Accessibility: Most hotels comply with ADA requirements; confirm specific needs when booking.
  • International visitors: Easy Metro access to D.C. attractions; many hotels have multilingual staff.

Pro Tip:

If visiting in summer, choose a hotel with a pool to escape the heat and humidity. Many business-oriented hotels have excellent fitness facilities too.

8. Tips for Finding the Best Deals

Save money on your Alexandria stay with these strategies:

  • Book directly through hotel websites for potential member discounts or perks
  • Consider Sunday-Thursday stays for business hotels that cater to weekday guests
  • Look for packages that include parking (valuable in Old Town)
  • Check government/military rates if eligible
  • Use price comparison tools but verify cancellation policies
  • Follow hotels on social media for flash sales
  • Consider Landmark/Van Dorn area hotels for better value

9. Local Amenities and Services

Alexandria hotels offer convenient access to:

  • Dining: From casual to fine dining, especially along King Street
  • Shopping: Old Town boutiques, Potomac Yard retail center
  • Grocery stores: Whole Foods, Trader Joe's, and Safeway locations
  • Pharmacies: Multiple CVS and Walgreens locations
  • Fitness centers: Many hotels have gyms; local gyms may offer guest passes
  • Business services: FedEx Office locations and hotel business centers
