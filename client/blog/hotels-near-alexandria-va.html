Your Complete Guide to Hotels in Alexandria, Virginia

Alexandria, Virginia, with its rich history, charming Old Town, and proximity to Washington D.C., offers a wide range of hotel options for every type of traveler. Whether you're visiting for business, leisure, or a bit of both, this guide will help you navigate the hotel landscape in this beautiful Potomac River city.

1. Popular Hotel Districts and Neighborhoods

Alexandria's hotels are concentrated in several key areas, each with its own character and advantages:

  • Old Town Alexandria - The historic heart of the city with cobblestone streets, boutique shops, and waterfront views. Hotels here tend to be more upscale.
  • Carlyle District - Near the federal courthouse and Eisenhower Avenue Metro, popular with business travelers.
  • Landmark/Van Dorn - More affordable options near I-395 and the Van Dorn Street Metro station.
  • Potomac Yard - A developing area with newer hotels, near the Potomac Yard Metro and shopping centers.
  • West End - Budget-friendly options near I-495 (Capital Beltway), convenient for those with cars.

2. Average Hotel Prices and Seasonal Variations

Hotel prices in Alexandria vary significantly by season, location, and type of accommodation:

  • Budget hotels: $90-$150 per night
  • Mid-range hotels: $150-$250 per night
  • Luxury hotels: $250-$400+ per night
  • Boutique hotels: $200-$350 per night

Peak seasons (highest prices): Spring (especially cherry blossom season in late March/early April), summer (June-August), and fall foliage season (October).

Shoulder seasons (moderate prices): Late April-May, September.

Low season (best deals): January-February (excluding holiday weekends) and December (after the holiday rush).

3. Types of Hotels Available

Alexandria offers diverse lodging options to suit all preferences and budgets:

  • Luxury Hotels: The Alexandrian (Autograph Collection), Lorien Hotel & Spa, Morrison House (Autograph Collection)
  • Boutique Hotels: Hotel Indigo Old Town Alexandria, The Westin Alexandria Old Town
  • Business Hotels: Hilton Alexandria Mark Center, Sheraton Suites Old Town Alexandria
  • Budget Hotels: Comfort Inn Alexandria, Holiday Inn Express Alexandria - Fort Belvoir
  • Extended Stay: Residence Inn Alexandria Old Town South, Homewood Suites by Hilton Alexandria
  • Historic Inns: The Alexandrian, Morrison House

4. Nearby Attractions and Points of Interest

Alexandria's hotels put you close to numerous attractions:

  • Old Town Waterfront: Charming shops, restaurants, and scenic views along the Potomac
  • Torpedo Factory Art Center: Working artist studios in a converted WWII torpedo factory
  • George Washington's Mount Vernon: The historic estate of America's first president
  • King Street Mile: A vibrant stretch of boutiques, galleries, and eateries
  • Gadsby's Tavern Museum: Historic 18th-century tavern frequented by founding fathers
  • D.C. Attractions: Just minutes from the National Mall, Smithsonian museums, and monuments

5. Transportation Options and Accessibility

Getting around Alexandria and to Washington D.C. is convenient:

  • Metro: Blue and Yellow Lines serve Alexandria with stations at King Street, Eisenhower Avenue, and Van Dorn Street
  • DASH Buses: Alexandria's free local bus system with routes throughout the city
  • Water Taxi: Seasonal service between Old Town and D.C. attractions like The Wharf and Georgetown
  • Biking: Bike share stations and the Mount Vernon Trail along the Potomac
  • Ride-sharing: Uber and Lyft are widely available
  • Airports: Reagan National Airport (DCA) is just 4 miles away; Dulles (IAD) is about 25 miles

6. Best Times to Visit

The ideal time to visit Alexandria depends on your priorities:

  • For pleasant weather: April-May or September-October
  • For festivals: Summer (especially July 4th) or December (holiday events)
  • For fewer crowds: January-February
  • For cherry blossoms: Late March to early April (but book early!)
  • For budget travelers: Winter months (excluding holiday periods)

7. Special Considerations for Travelers

  • Business travelers: Many hotels near the Metro or in Carlyle District offer business centers and meeting spaces
  • Families: Look for hotels with pools and suites; some offer "kids stay free" policies
  • Pet owners: Several pet-friendly hotels (often with fees); check policies in advance
  • Accessibility: Most hotels comply with ADA requirements; confirm specific needs when booking
  • International visitors: Many hotels have multilingual staff; proximity to DCA airport is convenient

Pro Tip:

If you're attending an event at the National Harbor, consider Alexandria hotels as an often more affordable alternative with easy access via water taxi or Metro.
