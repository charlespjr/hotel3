Your Complete Guide to Hotels in Omaha, Nebraska

Omaha, Nebraska's largest city, offers visitors a blend of Midwestern charm, cultural attractions, and excellent hospitality. Whether you're visiting for business, leisure, or a special event, choosing the right hotel can make all the difference in your experience. This comprehensive guide covers everything you need to know about hotels in Omaha.

1. Popular Hotel Districts and Neighborhoods

Omaha has several key areas where hotels are concentrated, each offering distinct advantages:

  • Downtown/Old Market: The heart of Omaha with historic charm, upscale hotels, and walkable access to dining and entertainment.
  • Midtown: A mix of business and leisure hotels near major hospitals and cultural institutions.
  • West Omaha: Suburban area with newer hotels, often near shopping centers and corporate offices.
  • Airport Area: Convenient for business travelers with many mid-range chain hotels.
  • Council Bluffs, IA: Just across the river with casino hotels and often lower rates.

2. Average Hotel Prices and Seasonal Variations

Hotel prices in Omaha vary by season, location, and type:

  • Budget hotels: $70-$120 per night
  • Mid-range hotels: $120-$200 per night
  • Luxury hotels: $200-$400+ per night

Seasonal variations:

  • Peak seasons: Summer (June-August) and during major events like the College World Series (June) or Berkshire Hathaway shareholders meeting (May)
  • Shoulder seasons: Spring (April-May) and Fall (September-October) often offer pleasant weather and lower rates
  • Low season: Winter (November-March) has the lowest prices but coldest weather

3. Types of Hotels Available

Omaha offers diverse lodging options to suit every preference and budget:

  • Luxury Hotels: The Magnolia Hotel, Kimpton Cottonwood Hotel, The Peregrine Omaha Downtown
  • Boutique Hotels: Hotel Deco XV, The Farnam Hotel
  • Business Hotels: Omaha Marriott Downtown, Hilton Omaha, Hyatt Place Omaha/Downtown-Old Market
  • Budget Options: Microtel Inn & Suites, Motel 6, various chain hotels near the airport
  • Extended Stay: Residence Inn, Homewood Suites, Candlewood Suites
  • Casino Hotels: Horseshoe Council Bluffs, Harrah's Council Bluffs (just across the river in Iowa)

4. Nearby Attractions and Points of Interest

Omaha boasts numerous attractions that influence hotel choices:

  • Henry Doorly Zoo and Aquarium: Consistently ranked among the world's best zoos
  • Old Market District: Historic area with cobblestone streets, shops, and restaurants
  • CHI Health Center: Major convention and event venue
  • TD Ameritrade Park: Home of the College World Series
  • Joslyn Art Museum: Impressive collection of art spanning centuries
  • Bob Kerrey Pedestrian Bridge: Scenic bridge connecting Nebraska and Iowa
  • Durham Museum: Housed in Omaha's beautiful former Union Station

5. Transportation Options and Accessibility

Getting around Omaha from your hotel:

  • Public Transit: Metro Transit buses serve most areas ($1.75 per ride)
  • Ride-sharing: Uber and Lyft widely available
  • Taxi Services: Several local companies operate in the metro area
  • Rental Cars: Available at the airport and downtown locations
  • Bike Share: Heartland Bike Share program with stations throughout the city
  • Accessibility: Most hotels comply with ADA requirements; confirm specific needs when booking

6. Best Times to Visit

The ideal time to visit Omaha depends on your priorities:

  • For pleasant weather: May-June or September-October offer mild temperatures
  • For events: June for the College World Series, May for Berkshire Hathaway meeting
  • For lowest prices: January-February (but be prepared for cold weather)
  • For outdoor activities: Late spring through early fall

7. Special Considerations for Travelers

  • Business travelers: Downtown hotels offer proximity to the business district and convention center
  • Families: Look for hotels near the zoo with pool facilities
  • Pet owners: Several hotels are pet-friendly (Kimpton Cottonwood, Aloft Omaha)
  • Accessibility needs: Most major hotels offer ADA-compliant rooms; confirm specifics when booking
  • Extended stays: Consider hotels with kitchenettes or nearby grocery options

8. Tips for Finding the Best Deals

Follow these strategies to save on Omaha hotels:

  • Book midweek when business travel slows (Tuesday-Thursday often cheapest)
  • Check hotel websites for direct booking discounts
  • Look for package deals that include zoo tickets or parking
  • Consider Council Bluffs hotels for lower rates (just across the river)
  • Use price comparison tools but check the hotel's own site for price matching
  • Sign up for hotel loyalty programs for potential upgrades
  • Travel during shoulder seasons (spring and fall) for better rates

9. Local Amenities and Services

Omaha hotels typically offer or are near:

  • Dining: From steak houses (Omaha is famous for its beef) to diverse international cuisine
  • Shopping: Westroads Mall, Village Pointe, and the Old Market's boutique shops
  • Fitness: Many hotels have gyms; downtown has several public recreation options
  • Business Services: Most business hotels offer meeting rooms and business centers
