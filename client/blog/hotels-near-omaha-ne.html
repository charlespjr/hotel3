Your Complete Guide to Hotels in Omaha, Nebraska

Omaha, Nebraska's largest city, offers visitors a blend of Midwestern charm, vibrant cultural attractions, and excellent hospitality options. Whether you're visiting for business, a family vacation, or a weekend getaway, this guide will help you navigate Omaha's hotel scene.

1. Popular Hotel Districts and Neighborhoods

Omaha's hotels are concentrated in several key areas that cater to different types of travelers:

Downtown Omaha

The heart of the city, ideal for business travelers and those wanting to be near major attractions like the Old Market and CHI Health Center.

Old Market

A historic district with boutique hotels, charming brick streets, and excellent dining options.

Midtown

Close to the University of Nebraska Medical Center and several cultural institutions.

West Omaha

Features many chain hotels near shopping centers and corporate offices, often with more affordable rates.

Airport Area

Convenient for early flights or short stays, with several budget-friendly options.

2. Average Hotel Prices and Seasonal Variations

Hotel prices in Omaha vary by season and location:

  • Budget hotels: $70-$120 per night
  • Mid-range hotels: $120-$200 per night
  • Luxury hotels: $200-$400+ per night

Seasonal variations:

  • Peak seasons: Summer (June-August) and during major events like the College World Series (June)
  • Shoulder seasons: Spring (April-May) and Fall (September-October) offer pleasant weather and lower rates
  • Low season: Winter (November-March), except around holidays, when prices can drop significantly

3. Types of Hotels Available

Omaha offers accommodation options for every preference and budget:

Luxury Hotels

  • The Magnolia Hotel Omaha (boutique luxury)
  • Kimpton Cottonwood Hotel (historic charm with modern amenities)
  • Hilton Omaha (connected to the CHI Health Center)

Boutique Hotels

  • The Peregrine Omaha Downtown (artsy vibe in the Old Market)
  • Hotel Deco XV (Art Deco style downtown)

Budget Hotels

  • Various chain options like Motel 6, Super 8, and Econo Lodge
  • Microtel Inn & Suites by Wyndham

Extended Stay Options

  • Homewood Suites by Hilton
  • Staybridge Suites
  • Candlewood Suites

4. Nearby Attractions and Points of Interest

Omaha offers diverse attractions that influence hotel choices:

  • Henry Doorly Zoo and Aquarium: Consistently ranked among the world's best zoos
  • The Old Market: Historic district with shops, galleries, and restaurants
  • CHI Health Center Omaha: Major convention and event venue
  • TD Ameritrade Park: Home of the College World Series
  • Joslyn Art Museum: Impressive collection of art spanning centuries
  • Bob Kerrey Pedestrian Bridge: Scenic bridge connecting Nebraska and Iowa
  • Durham Museum: Housed in Omaha's beautiful former Union Station

5. Transportation Options and Accessibility

Getting around Omaha from your hotel:

Public Transportation

  • Metro Transit buses serve most areas (single ride $1.25)
  • ORBT (Omaha Rapid Bus Transit) runs along Dodge Street

Ride Sharing

  • Uber and Lyft operate throughout the city

Car Rentals

  • Available at Eppley Airfield and downtown locations
  • Major companies include Enterprise, Hertz, and Avis

Accessibility

  • Most hotels comply with ADA requirements
  • Downtown hotels are most walkable
  • Some hotels offer shuttle services to major attractions

6. Best Times to Visit

The ideal time to visit Omaha depends on your priorities:

  • For events: June (College World Series), July (Summer Arts Festival), September (Omaha Beer Fest)
  • For pleasant weather: May-June and September-October
  • For budget travelers: January-March (excluding holiday periods)
  • For zoo visits: Spring or fall to avoid extreme temperatures

7. Special Considerations for Travelers

Business Travelers

  • Downtown hotels offer best access to business centers
  • Many hotels have business centers and meeting rooms
  • Weekday rates may be higher than weekends

Families

  • Look for hotels near the zoo with pool facilities
  • Suites with kitchenettes can be cost-effective
  • Check for family packages at attractions

Pet Owners

  • Several pet-friendly hotels available (typically with fees)
  • Popular options include Aloft Omaha West and Element Omaha Midtown Crossing

8. Tips for Finding the Best Deals

Money-Saving Strategies

  • Book directly with hotels for potential discounts
  • Sign up for hotel loyalty programs
  • Check for package deals that include attraction tickets
  • Consider last-minute booking apps for same-day deals
  • Travel during shoulder seasons for lower rates
