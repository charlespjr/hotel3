The Ultimate Guide to Hotels in Las Vegas, NV

Las Vegas, the Entertainment Capital of the World, offers an incredible variety of hotel options to suit every taste and budget. Whether you're looking for luxury resorts, budget-friendly accommodations, or unique boutique experiences, Sin City has it all. This comprehensive guide covers everything you need to know about staying in Las Vegas.

1. Popular Hotel Districts and Neighborhoods

Las Vegas hotels are concentrated in several key areas:

  • The Las Vegas Strip: The 4.2-mile stretch of Las Vegas Boulevard South that features most of the city's iconic resorts like Bellagio, Caesars Palace, and The Venetian.
  • Downtown Las Vegas: The original gambling district featuring classic casinos like Golden Nugget and Fremont Street Experience.
  • Off-Strip Hotels: More affordable options located near but not on the Strip, such as The Orleans and Palms Casino Resort.
  • Summerlin: A suburban area with upscale hotels like Red Rock Casino Resort.
  • Henderson: Southeast of the Strip, home to Green Valley Ranch and other local favorites.

2. Average Hotel Prices and Seasonal Variations

Hotel prices in Las Vegas fluctuate dramatically based on season and events:

  • Budget hotels: $30-$80 per night (weekdays), $60-$150 (weekends)
  • Mid-range hotels: $80-$200 per night (weekdays), $150-$350 (weekends)
  • Luxury hotels: $200-$500+ per night (weekdays), $400-$1000+ (weekends)

Peak seasons (highest prices) include New Year's Eve, CES (January), March Madness, Memorial Day weekend, Labor Day weekend, and major fight weekends.

Best value seasons are typically January (after CES), late August, September (except Labor Day), and December (before Christmas).

3. Types of Hotels Available

Las Vegas offers accommodation options for every preference:

  • Luxury Resorts: Wynn, Encore, Bellagio, The Venetian, Palazzo, Aria, Cosmopolitan
  • Budget Hotels: Excalibur, Circus Circus, Stratosphere, Flamingo
  • Boutique Hotels: The Cromwell, Nobu Hotel, Delano Las Vegas
  • All-Suite Hotels: Signature at MGM Grand, Trump International, The Palazzo
  • Off-Strip Value Options: Orleans, Gold Coast, Palace Station
  • Extended Stay: Homewood Suites, Residence Inn, Siegel Suites

4. Nearby Attractions and Points of Interest

Beyond the casinos, Las Vegas offers incredible attractions:

  • On the Strip: Bellagio Fountains, High Roller Observation Wheel, Mirage Volcano, Venetian Gondola Rides
  • Shows & Entertainment: Cirque du Soleil shows, magic shows (Penn & Teller, David Copperfield), residencies (Adele, Garth Brooks)
  • Downtown: Fremont Street Experience, Neon Museum, Mob Museum
  • Nearby: Red Rock Canyon, Hoover Dam, Grand Canyon (day trips), Valley of Fire State Park
  • Dining: Celebrity chef restaurants (Gordon Ramsay, Wolfgang Puck), buffets (Bacchanal, Wicked Spoon)

5. Transportation Options and Accessibility

Getting around Las Vegas is relatively easy:

  • Monorail: Runs along the east side of the Strip with 7 stations
  • Rideshare/Taxis: Uber and Lyft operate throughout the city
  • Deuce Bus: Double-decker bus that runs the length of the Strip 24/7
  • Walking: Many hotels are connected via pedestrian bridges
  • Rental Cars: Available but often unnecessary for Strip visitors
  • Accessibility: Most hotels are ADA compliant with wheelchair access

6. Best Times to Visit

The ideal time to visit depends on your priorities:

  • Best weather: March-May and September-November (70s-80sÂ°F)
  • Lowest prices: January (post-New Year's), July-August (hot), December (pre-holidays)
  • Special events:
    • January: CES (huge tech convention)
    • March: March Madness, NASCAR Weekend
    • May: Electric Daisy Carnival (EDC)
    • July: Independence Day celebrations
    • November: Las Vegas Grand Prix (F1)
    • December: National Finals Rodeo

7. Special Considerations for Travelers

  • Resort fees: Nearly all hotels charge daily resort fees ($20-$50) not included in advertised rates
  • Parking fees: Most Strip hotels now charge for parking ($15-$35 per day)
  • Age restrictions: Must be 21+ to check into most hotels with casinos
  • Smoking: Many hotels have smoking rooms and allow smoking in casinos
  • Large conventions: Can cause price spikes and crowded conditions
  • Wedding chapels: Many hotels have on-site wedding services

Pro Tip:

Always check the hotel's exact location - some "Las Vegas" hotels are actually in adjacent cities like Paradise or Winchester, which might affect transportation options.

8. Tips for Finding the Best Deals

  1. Book midweek stays (Tuesday-Thursday) for the lowest rates
  2. Sign up for hotel loyalty programs (MGM Rewards, Caesars Rewards)
  3. Check aggregator sites but also visit hotel websites directly
  4. Look for package deals that include show tickets or dining credits
  5. Consider last-minute bookings (but risky during peak times)
  6. Follow Las Vegas deals accounts on social media
  7. Ask about comps if you gamble (even small amounts)
  8. Check for convention blackout dates before booking
