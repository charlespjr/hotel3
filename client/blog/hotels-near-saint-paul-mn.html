Your Complete Guide to Hotels in Saint Paul, Minnesota

Saint Paul, the capital city of Minnesota, offers visitors a charming blend of historic architecture, cultural attractions, and Midwestern hospitality. Whether you're visiting for business, leisure, or a special event, choosing the right hotel can make all the difference in your experience. This comprehensive guide covers everything you need to know about hotels in Saint Paul.

1. Popular Hotel Districts and Neighborhoods

Saint Paul has several key areas where hotels are concentrated, each with its own character and advantages:

  • Downtown Saint Paul - The heart of the city with proximity to government buildings, entertainment venues, and the Mississippi River
  • Midway - Convenient location between Minneapolis and Saint Paul with good transit access
  • Highland Park - A more residential area with boutique options near the Mississippi River
  • Near the Airport (MSP) - Many chain hotels offering easy access to air travel
  • West Seventh - Historic neighborhood with charming smaller hotels near cultural attractions

2. Average Hotel Prices and Seasonal Variations

Hotel prices in Saint Paul vary significantly by season, with the highest demand during:

  • Summer months (June-August) - Average rates: $150-$300/night
  • Winter holiday season (December) - Average rates: $120-$250/night
  • Spring and fall - Average rates: $100-$200/night

Budget hotels typically start around $80/night, while luxury properties can exceed $400/night during peak periods. Weekdays (especially Tuesday-Thursday) often offer better rates than weekends.

3. Types of Hotels Available

Saint Paul offers accommodation options for every taste and budget:

  • Luxury Hotels: The Saint Paul Hotel, InterContinental Saint Paul Riverfront
  • Boutique Hotels: The Lexington, Celeste St. Paul
  • Budget Hotels: Super 8, Motel 6, and various independent motels
  • Extended Stay: Residence Inn, Homewood Suites, Candlewood Suites
  • Business Hotels: Hilton Garden Inn, Courtyard by Marriott
  • Historic Hotels: The Saint Paul Hotel (since 1910), The Lexington

4. Nearby Attractions and Points of Interest

Many Saint Paul hotels are conveniently located near major attractions:

  • Minnesota State Capitol
  • Science Museum of Minnesota
  • Xcel Energy Center (home of the Minnesota Wild)
  • Cathedral of Saint Paul
  • Mississippi River waterfront
  • Landmark Center
  • Minnesota History Center
  • Como Park Zoo & Conservatory
  • James J. Hill House

5. Transportation Options and Accessibility

Getting around Saint Paul is relatively easy with these options:

  • Light Rail: Metro Green Line connects Saint Paul to Minneapolis
  • Buses: Metro Transit operates extensive bus routes
  • Ride-sharing: Uber and Lyft widely available
  • Taxis: Traditional taxi services operate throughout the city
  • Biking: Nice Ride bike share program and extensive trails
  • Airport Access: MSP International Airport is about 15-20 minutes from most Saint Paul hotels

Most downtown hotels are ADA compliant with accessible rooms available. It's recommended to confirm specific accessibility needs when booking.

6. Best Times to Visit

The ideal time to visit Saint Paul depends on your preferences:

  • Summer (June-August): Warm weather, festivals, and outdoor activities (peak season)
  • Fall (September-October): Beautiful foliage, mild temperatures, and fewer crowds
  • Winter (December-February): Cold but festive, with holiday events and winter sports
  • Spring (April-May): Cooler temperatures but lower hotel rates

Special events like the Winter Carnival (January-February) and the Minnesota State Fair (late August-early September) can affect hotel availability and prices.

7. Special Considerations for Travelers

  • Business Travelers: Downtown hotels offer best access to government and corporate offices
  • Families:
    • Look for hotels with pools and free breakfast
    • Como Park area is great for families with young children
  • Accessibility Needs: Most major hotels offer ADA-compliant rooms (request when booking)
  • Pet Owners: Several hotels are pet-friendly (usually with fees)
  • International Visitors: Downtown hotels typically have staff who speak multiple languages

8. Tips for Finding the Best Deals

Money-Saving Strategies:

  • Book directly with hotels for potential discounts and perks
  • Consider package deals that include parking or attraction tickets
  • Sign up for hotel loyalty programs for member rates
  • Check for government or corporate rates if applicable
  • Look for last-minute deals on hotel booking apps
  • Visit during shoulder seasons (spring and fall) for lower rates
  • Consider staying just outside downtown for better value

9. Local Amenities and Services

Most Saint Paul hotels offer convenient amenities:

  • Dining: Many hotels have on-site restaurants or are near excellent local eateries
  • Fitness Centers: Standard at most mid-range and upscale hotels
  • Business Centers: Available at business-oriented hotels
  • Parking: Most hotels offer parking (often for a daily fee)
  • Concierge Services: Available at higher-end properties

Downtown Saint Paul has excellent walkability with pharmacies, coffee shops, and convenience stores nearby most hotels.
