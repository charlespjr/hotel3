Your Complete Guide to Hotels in St. Louis, Missouri

St. Louis, the Gateway to the West, offers visitors a rich blend of history, culture, and modern attractions. Whether you're visiting for business or pleasure, finding the right hotel can make all the difference in your experience. This comprehensive guide covers everything you need to know about hotels in St. Louis.

1. Popular Hotel Districts and Neighborhoods

St. Louis has several distinct areas where hotels are concentrated, each offering different advantages:

Downtown St. Louis

The heart of the city, home to major attractions like the Gateway Arch and Busch Stadium. Hotels here range from luxury to budget options.

Central West End

An upscale neighborhood with boutique hotels, trendy restaurants, and proximity to Forest Park.

Clayton

St. Louis's business district with many upscale hotels catering to corporate travelers.

The Loop/Delmar

Near Washington University, this area has more budget-friendly options with a youthful vibe.

Midtown

Close to cultural institutions like the Fox Theatre and Saint Louis University.

Airport Area

Convenient for short stays or early flights, with many chain hotels offering shuttle service.

2. Average Hotel Prices and Seasonal Variations

Hotel prices in St. Louis vary significantly by season, location, and hotel class:

  • Budget hotels: $70-$120 per night
  • Mid-range hotels: $120-$220 per night
  • Luxury hotels: $220-$400+ per night

Seasonal variations:

  • Peak season (April-October): Higher prices, especially during baseball season and summer festivals
  • Shoulder season (March & November): Moderate prices with pleasant weather
  • Off-season (December-February): Lowest prices, though some attractions may have reduced hours

Pro Tip: Hotel prices often spike during Cardinals home games, major conventions, and special events like the Fair Saint Louis (July 4th weekend).

4. Nearby Attractions and Points of Interest

St. Louis boasts numerous attractions near major hotel districts:

Downtown Area

  • Busch Stadium (St. Louis Cardinals)

5. Transportation Options and Accessibility

Getting around St. Louis from your hotel:

Public Transportation

  • MetroLink light rail connects airport, downtown, and key areas
  • MetroBus system covers most of the city
  • Free trolley circulates in downtown during peak hours

Ride Services

  • Uber and Lyft widely available
  • Traditional taxi services

Driving

  • Most hotels offer parking (often for a fee downtown)
  • Rental car agencies available at airport and downtown

Accessibility

Most major hotels comply with ADA requirements, but it's always best to confirm specific needs when booking.

6. Best Times to Visit

The ideal time to visit St. Louis depends on your priorities:

  • Spring (April-May): Pleasant temperatures, blooming gardens, baseball season begins
  • Summer (June-August): Festival season, but can be hot and humid
  • Fall (September-October): Comfortable weather, beautiful foliage, fewer crowds
  • Winter (November-February): Lowest hotel rates, holiday decorations, but some outdoor attractions closed

Business Travelers

Families
