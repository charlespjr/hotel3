The Ultimate Guide to Hotels in Plano, Texas

Plano, Texas, located just north of Dallas, is a thriving city known for its excellent schools, corporate headquarters, and family-friendly atmosphere. Whether you're visiting for business, leisure, or a special event, choosing the right hotel can make all the difference in your experience. This comprehensive guide covers everything you need to know about hotels in Plano.

1. Popular Hotel Districts and Neighborhoods

Plano has several key areas where hotels are concentrated, each offering unique advantages:

  • Legacy West/Plano Parkway: The premier business and upscale shopping district with luxury hotels near corporate offices and high-end retail.
  • Downtown Plano: Historic area with boutique hotels near the DART rail line and charming local restaurants.
  • US-75 Corridor: Convenient location near major highways with a mix of mid-range and budget hotels.
  • Shops at Legacy/Legacy Drive: Upscale area with hotels near popular dining and entertainment options.
  • West Plano (Preston Road): Family-friendly area with hotels near parks and residential neighborhoods.

2. Average Hotel Prices and Seasonal Variations

Hotel prices in Plano vary significantly based on season, location, and type of hotel:

  • Budget hotels: $70-$120 per night
  • Mid-range hotels: $120-$200 per night
  • Luxury hotels: $200-$400+ per night

Seasonal variations:

  • Peak seasons (highest prices): Spring (March-May) and Fall (September-November)
  • Shoulder seasons: Summer (June-August) - prices moderate but can spike during special events
  • Low season (best deals): Winter (December-February), excluding holidays

3. Types of Hotels Available

Plano offers accommodation options for every taste and budget:

  • Luxury Hotels: The Renaissance Dallas at Plano Legacy West, The Westin Stonebriar Golf Resort & Spa
  • Business Hotels: Dallas/Plano Marriott at Legacy Town Center, Aloft Plano
  • Budget Hotels: La Quinta by Wyndham Plano, Motel 6 Plano, TX - Dallas North
  • Boutique Hotels: Hotel Preston (unique artistic vibe), The Artisan
  • Extended Stay: Residence Inn Plano Richardson, Homewood Suites by Hilton Dallas/Plano
  • Family-Friendly: Great Wolf Lodge (indoor waterpark), Hyatt House Dallas/Plano

4. Nearby Attractions and Points of Interest

Plano hotels put you close to many attractions:

  • Shopping: The Shops at Legacy, Legacy West, Willow Bend Mall
  • Dining: Hundreds of restaurants from casual to fine dining
  • Entertainment: Legacy Hall food hall, Crayola Experience, Plano Super Bowl
  • Cultural: The Interurban Railway Museum, Heritage Farmstead Museum
  • Outdoors: Arbor Hills Nature Preserve, Oak Point Park & Nature Preserve
  • Nearby: Dallas Arboretum (20 min), Frisco's Star (15 min), DFW Airport (30 min)

5. Transportation Options and Accessibility

Getting around Plano and the Dallas area:

  • DART Rail: Connects Plano to Dallas via the Red Line (stations in Downtown Plano and Parker Road)
  • Ride-sharing: Uber and Lyft widely available
  • Car Rentals: Available at most hotels and DFW Airport
  • Highways: Easy access to US-75, Sam Rayburn Tollway, George Bush Turnpike
  • Airports: DFW International (30-45 min) and Dallas Love Field (35-50 min)
  • Accessibility: Most hotels comply with ADA requirements; confirm specific needs when booking

6. Best Times to Visit

The ideal time to visit Plano depends on your priorities:

  • Weather: Spring (April-May) and Fall (October-November) offer mild temperatures
  • Events: Plano Balloon Festival (September), Plano International Festival (October)
  • Low crowds: January-February (except Super Bowl weekend if in Dallas)
  • Business travel: Avoid major convention weeks in Dallas (check Dallas calendar)

7. Special Considerations for Travelers

  • Business travelers: Look for hotels with business centers, meeting rooms, and proximity to corporate offices
  • Families: Consider hotels with pools, suites, and kid-friendly amenities
  • Extended stays: Seek hotels with kitchenettes and laundry facilities
  • Pet owners: Many Plano hotels are pet-friendly (usually with fees)
  • Accessibility needs: Confirm specific accommodations before booking

Pro Tip:

If attending an event at the Star in Frisco or Toyota Stadium, consider staying in West Plano for shorter commute and better prices than Frisco hotels.

8. Tips for Finding the Best Deals

  • Book directly with hotels for potential perks like free upgrades or breakfast
  • Check corporate rates if traveling for business
  • Look for package deals that include parking or dining credits
  • Consider weekends for business hotels (lower rates when corporate travelers leave)
  • Use price comparison tools but verify amenities and cancellation policies
  • Sign up for hotel loyalty programs for member-only rates
  • Check for Texas resident discounts if applicable
