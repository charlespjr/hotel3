Hotels in Lexington, KY – A Complete Guide

Lexington, Kentucky, known as the "Horse Capital of the World," offers a mix of Southern charm, rich history, and modern amenities. Whether you're visiting for business, a horse race, or exploring the Bourbon Trail, finding the right hotel is key to a great trip. This guide covers everything you need to know about Lexington hotels.

1. Popular Hotel Districts and Neighborhoods

Lexington has several key areas where hotels are concentrated, each offering a unique experience:

  • Downtown Lexington – The heart of the city, close to entertainment, dining, and Rupp Arena.
  • Hamburg Area – A commercial hub with shopping centers and chain hotels.
  • Nicholasville Road Corridor – Convenient for budget travelers with easy highway access.
  • Near Keeneland Race Course – Ideal for horse racing fans.
  • The Summit at Fritz Farm – Upscale shopping and boutique hotel options.

2. Average Hotel Prices and Seasonal Variations

Hotel prices in Lexington vary by season and location:

  • Budget hotels: $70–$120 per night
  • Mid-range hotels: $120–$200 per night
  • Luxury hotels: $200–$400+ per night

Peak seasons (higher prices):

  • Keeneland Race Meets (April & October)
  • University of Kentucky football/basketball season
  • Summer tourism (June–August)

Best value seasons: January–February and late November (excluding Thanksgiving).

3. Types of Hotels Available

Lexington offers accommodations for every preference and budget:

  • Luxury Hotels: The Campbell House (Curio Collection by Hilton), 21c Museum Hotel
  • Boutique Hotels: The Manchester Hotel, Gratz Park Inn
  • Budget-Friendly Chains: Red Roof Inn, Motel 6, Super 8
  • Extended Stay: Homewood Suites, Residence Inn
  • Historic Inns: Lyndon House Bed & Breakfast

4. Nearby Attractions and Points of Interest

Top attractions near Lexington hotels:

  • Keeneland Race Course – Iconic horse racing venue
  • Kentucky Horse Park – Equestrian museum and events
  • Bourbon Trail Distilleries – Woodford Reserve, Buffalo Trace
  • Rupp Arena – Home of UK Wildcats basketball
  • Mary Todd Lincoln House – Historic presidential site
  • The Arboretum – Beautiful botanical gardens

5. Transportation Options and Accessibility

Getting around Lexington:

  • Blue Grass Airport (LEX) – 6 miles west of downtown
  • Lextran Public Transit – Bus service throughout the city
  • Ride-sharing – Uber and Lyft widely available
  • Taxi Services – Several local companies operate
  • Bike Share – LouVelo bike rental program
  • Major Highways: I-64, I-75, and New Circle Road (Route 4)

Most downtown hotels are walkable to restaurants and attractions.

6. Best Times to Visit

Ideal times to visit Lexington depend on your interests:

  • Spring (April–May): Pleasant weather, Keeneland spring meet
  • Fall (September–October): Beautiful foliage, Keeneland fall meet
  • Summer (June–August): Warm weather, outdoor festivals
  • Winter (December): Holiday events, lower hotel rates

7. Special Considerations for Travelers

  • Accessibility: Most hotels comply with ADA requirements
  • Pet-Friendly Options: Many hotels allow pets (usually with fee)
  • Business Travelers: Look for hotels with meeting spaces and business centers
  • Families: Seek hotels with pools and free breakfast
  • Bourbon Trail Visitors: Consider hotels with shuttle services

8. Tips for Finding the Best Deals

Money-Saving Strategies:

  • Book directly through hotel websites for best rates
  • Sign up for hotel loyalty programs
  • Check for package deals (hotel + attraction tickets)
  • Travel during off-peak times
  • Look for last-minute deals on apps like HotelTonight
  • Consider hotels slightly outside downtown for better value

9. Local Amenities and Services

What to expect from Lexington hotels:

  • Dining: Many hotels have restaurants featuring Kentucky cuisine
  • Parking: Most hotels offer parking (some charge daily fees)
  • WiFi: Nearly all hotels provide free WiFi
  • Fitness Centers: Common in mid-range and upscale hotels
  • Concierge Services: Available at luxury properties

10. Safety and Security Information

Lexington is generally safe, but take normal precautions:

  • Downtown and major hotel areas are well-patrolled
  • Use hotel safes for valuables
  • Park in well-lit areas at night
  • Most hotels have 24-hour front desk staff
  • Check recent reviews for any security concerns
