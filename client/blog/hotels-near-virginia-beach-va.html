The Ultimate Guide to Hotels in Virginia Beach, VA

Virginia Beach is one of the most popular coastal destinations on the East Coast, offering miles of sandy beaches, vibrant nightlife, and family-friendly attractions. Whether you're planning a romantic getaway, family vacation, or business trip, choosing the right hotel can make all the difference. This comprehensive guide covers everything you need to know about hotels in Virginia Beach.

1. Popular Hotel Districts and Neighborhoods

Virginia Beach offers several distinct areas to stay, each with its own character and advantages:

  • The Oceanfront (Boardwalk Area): The most popular area with beachfront hotels, restaurants, and entertainment along the famous 3-mile boardwalk.
  • Sandbridge Beach: A quieter, more residential area south of the main resort area with luxury beach houses and a few small hotels.
  • Town Center: The urban hub of Virginia Beach with upscale hotels, shopping, and dining, but not on the beach.
  • Pungo: A rural area with charming bed and breakfasts, ideal for those seeking a countryside retreat.
  • Norfolk/Virginia Beach border: Convenient for business travelers with easy access to both cities and typically lower prices.

2. Average Hotel Prices and Seasonal Variations

Hotel prices in Virginia Beach vary significantly by season:

  • Peak Season (June-August): $200-$400/night for oceanfront hotels, $150-$250 for nearby hotels
  • Shoulder Season (April-May, September-October): $120-$250/night oceanfront, $90-$180 nearby
  • Off Season (November-March): $80-$150/night oceanfront, $60-$120 nearby

Weekend rates are typically 20-30% higher than weekday rates. Major events like the Neptune Festival or East Coast Surfing Championships can cause prices to spike.

3. Types of Hotels Available

Virginia Beach offers accommodations for every taste and budget:

  • Luxury Hotels: The Cavalier (historic landmark), Hilton Virginia Beach Oceanfront, Marriott Virginia Beach Oceanfront
  • Mid-Range Hotels: Hyatt Place Virginia Beach/Oceanfront, SpringHill Suites by Marriott, Holiday Inn & Suites North Beach
  • Budget Hotels: Econo Lodge Oceanfront, Travelodge by Wyndham, Days Inn by Wyndham
  • Boutique Hotels: The Barclay Towers Hotel, The Oceanfront Inn, The Belvedere Beach Resort
  • Extended Stay/Corporate Housing: Residence Inn, Homewood Suites, Candlewood Suites
  • Resorts: Virginia Beach Resort Hotel and Conference Center, Sandcastle Resort

4. Nearby Attractions and Points of Interest

Virginia Beach offers numerous attractions near most hotels:

  • Virginia Beach Boardwalk: 3-mile oceanfront promenade with entertainment, dining, and shops
  • First Landing State Park: Beautiful trails and beaches where English colonists first landed in 1607
  • Virginia Aquarium & Marine Science Center: Excellent family attraction with marine exhibits and boat tours
  • Military Aviation Museum: Impressive collection of World War I and II aircraft
  • Neptune Statue: Iconic 34-foot bronze statue at the oceanfront
  • Back Bay National Wildlife Refuge: Beautiful natural area for hiking and wildlife viewing
  • Ocean Breeze Waterpark: Popular summer attraction with water slides and pools

5. Transportation Options and Accessibility

Getting around Virginia Beach is relatively easy:

  • Norfolk International Airport (ORF): 20-30 minutes from most Virginia Beach hotels
  • Hampton Roads Transit (HRT): Public buses serve major areas but can be limited
  • Taxis/Rideshares: Uber and Lyft operate throughout the area
  • Bike Rentals: Available along the boardwalk and at many hotels
  • Trolley: Seasonal trolley service runs along Atlantic Avenue
  • Parking: Most hotels charge $10-$30/night for parking; street parking can be limited

Pro Tip: If you're staying at the Oceanfront, many attractions are within walking distance, reducing the need for a rental car.

6. Best Times to Visit

The ideal time to visit depends on your priorities:

  • For Beach Activities: June-August (warmest water, but most crowded)
  • For Mild Weather & Fewer Crowds: May or September-October
  • For Budget Travelers: November-April (cooler but significantly cheaper)
  • For Events: Check the calendar for popular events like the Neptune Festival (September) or East Coast Surfing Championships (August)

7. Special Considerations for Travelers

  • Families: Look for hotels with pools, kids' programs, and kitchenettes
  • Business Travelers: Town Center hotels offer best conference facilities and proximity to offices
  • Disabled Travelers: Many oceanfront hotels have ADA-compliant rooms; call ahead to confirm specific needs
  • Pet Owners: Several hotels are pet-friendly, often with size restrictions and additional fees
  • Military: Many hotels offer military discounts near Naval Air Station Oceana and Joint Expeditionary Base Little Creek

8. Tips for Finding the Best Deals

  1. Book early for summer weekends (some hotels sell out months in advance)
  2. Consider package deals that include attractions or dining credits
  3. Check hotel websites for exclusive online discounts
  4. Look for last-minute deals if traveling offseason or midweek
  5. Join hotel loyalty programs for member rates and perks
  6. Consider staying just a few blocks from the oceanfront for better rates
  7. Travel during shoulder seasons (spring/fall) for best combination of weather and prices
