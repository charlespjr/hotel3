Hotels in Virginia Beach, VA – A Complete Guide

Virginia Beach is a premier coastal destination known for its sandy shores, vibrant boardwalk, and diverse attractions. Whether you're planning a family vacation, a romantic getaway, or a business trip, choosing the right hotel is key to a great experience. This guide covers everything you need to know about hotels in Virginia Beach.

1. Popular Hotel Districts and Neighborhoods

Virginia Beach offers several distinct areas to stay, each with its own vibe:

  • The Oceanfront: The most popular area, featuring beachfront hotels, the Boardwalk, and lively entertainment.
  • Sandbridge: A quieter, residential area with luxury beach rentals and a more secluded feel.
  • Town Center: A modern urban district with upscale hotels, shopping, and dining—ideal for business travelers.
  • Chesapeake Bay (Chicks Beach): Offers calmer waters and mid-range hotels, great for families.
  • Pungo: A rural area with budget-friendly inns, perfect for those seeking a countryside retreat.

2. Average Hotel Prices and Seasonal Variations

Hotel rates in Virginia Beach fluctuate based on the season:

  • Peak Season (June-August): $200-$400/night for oceanfront hotels; budget hotels start at $100-$150.
  • Shoulder Season (April-May, September-October): $120-$250/night, with fewer crowds.
  • Off-Season (November-March): $80-$150/night, with deep discounts at luxury properties.

Weekend rates are typically higher than weekday stays.

3. Types of Hotels Available

Virginia Beach offers accommodations for every budget and preference:

  • Luxury: The Cavalier Hotel, Hilton Virginia Beach Oceanfront
  • Budget: Econo Lodge, Red Roof Inn
  • Boutique: The Barclay Towers, The Ocean Beach Club
  • Family-Friendly: Holiday Inn & Suites, Great Wolf Lodge
  • Extended Stay: Residence Inn, Homewood Suites

4. Nearby Attractions and Points of Interest

Staying in Virginia Beach means easy access to top attractions:

  • Virginia Beach Boardwalk (3 miles long with shops and restaurants)
  • First Landing State Park (hiking and kayaking)
  • Virginia Aquarium & Marine Science Center
  • Military Aviation Museum
  • Sandbridge Beach (less crowded alternative)

5. Transportation Options and Accessibility

Getting around Virginia Beach is convenient:

  • Airport: Norfolk International Airport (ORF) is 20 minutes away.
  • Public Transit: Hampton Roads Transit (HRT) buses and the VB Wave trolley serve tourist areas.
  • Rideshare/Taxis: Uber and Lyft operate throughout the city.
  • Biking: The Boardwalk and trails make cycling a great option.
  • Parking: Many hotels offer free parking, but Oceanfront spots may require fees.

6. Best Times to Visit

Consider these factors when planning your trip:

  • Summer (June-August): Best for beach activities but crowded.
  • Spring/Fall (April-May, September-October): Mild weather, fewer tourists.
  • Winter (November-March): Lowest prices, but some attractions may be closed.

Special events like the Neptune Festival (September) and Boardwalk Art Show (June) can impact hotel availability.

7. Special Considerations for Travelers

  • Families: Look for hotels with pools and kid-friendly amenities.
  • Pet Owners: Many hotels are pet-friendly (e.g., La Quinta, Sheraton).
  • ADA Accessibility: Most major hotels comply with ADA standards.
  • Military Travelers: Discounts often available near Naval Air Station Oceana.

8. Tips for Finding the Best Deals

  • Book early for summer stays (3-6 months in advance).
  • Use price comparison sites like Kayak or Booking.com.
  • Check hotel websites for exclusive promotions.
  • Consider package deals (flight + hotel) for additional savings.
  • Travel mid-week for lower rates.

9. Local Amenities and Services

Virginia Beach hotels often provide:

  • Free Wi-Fi
  • Beach equipment rentals (chairs, umbrellas)
  • On-site dining and bars
  • Shuttle services to attractions
  • Concierge services for tour bookings

10. Safety and Security Information

Virginia Beach is generally safe, but take standard precautions:

  • Lock valuables in hotel safes.
  • Swim only in designated areas with lifeguards.
  • Be cautious of jellyfish in warmer months.
  • Emergency services: Dial 911.

Most hotels have 24/7 front desk staff and security cameras.

Final Thoughts

Virginia Beach offers a wide range of accommodations to suit every traveler’s needs. Whether you prefer a luxury oceanfront resort or a budget-friendly motel, planning ahead will ensure a comfortable and enjoyable stay. Use this guide to find the perfect hotel for your Virginia Beach adventure!
