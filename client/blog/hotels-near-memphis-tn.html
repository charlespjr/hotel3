The Ultimate Guide to Hotels in Memphis, Tennessee

Memphis, the birthplace of blues and home of Graceland, offers visitors a rich cultural experience with its music history, delicious barbecue, and Southern hospitality. Choosing the right hotel can make your visit even more memorable. This comprehensive guide covers everything you need to know about hotels in Memphis.

1. Popular Hotel Districts and Neighborhoods

Memphis has several distinct areas where hotels are concentrated, each offering different advantages:

Downtown Memphis

The heart of the city with easy access to major attractions. Ideal for first-time visitors.

  • Beale Street Entertainment District
  • South Main Arts District
  • Riverside Drive along the Mississippi

Midtown

A more residential area with boutique hotels near Overton Square and Cooper-Young districts.

East Memphis

Business-oriented area with chain hotels, convenient to I-240 and the airport.

Graceland Area

Hotels clustered near Elvis Presley's famous home, about 9 miles from downtown.

2. Average Hotel Prices and Seasonal Variations

Hotel prices in Memphis vary significantly by season and location:

  • Budget hotels: $70-$120 per night
  • Mid-range hotels: $120-$220 per night
  • Luxury hotels: $220-$400+ per night

Peak seasons (higher prices):

  • April-May (Music festivals and good weather)
  • August (Elvis Week)
  • December (holiday events)

Value seasons (lower prices):

  • January-February (coldest months)
  • July (hot weather)
  • September-October (after summer rush)

4. Nearby Attractions and Points of Interest

Memphis offers world-class attractions near major hotel districts:

  • Downtown: Beale Street, National Civil Rights Museum, Memphis Rock 'n' Soul Museum
  • Graceland Area: Elvis Presley's Graceland, Guest House at Graceland
  • Midtown: Memphis Zoo, Overton Park, Brooks Museum of Art
  • Citywide: Stax Museum of American Soul Music, Sun Studio, Shelby Farms Park

5. Transportation Options and Accessibility

Getting Around Memphis

  • Memphis International Airport (MEM): 12 miles from downtown
  • Public Transit: MATA buses and trolleys (limited coverage)
  • Rideshares: Uber and Lyft widely available
  • Car Rentals: Recommended for exploring beyond downtown
  • Parking: Most downtown hotels charge $15-$30/night for parking

Accessibility

Most major hotels comply with ADA requirements. Downtown areas have curb cuts and accessible attractions. Call hotels in advance for specific accessibility needs.

6. Best Times to Visit

The ideal time to visit Memphis depends on your priorities:

  • Best weather: April-May and September-October (mild temperatures)
  • Music lovers: May (Beale Street Music Festival) or October (Memphis Music & Heritage Festival)
  • Elvis fans: August (Elvis Week anniversary events)
  • Budget travelers: January-February or July (lower rates)

7. Special Considerations for Travelers

  • Family Travel: Look for hotels with pools and near the zoo or Children's Museum
  • Business Travel: East Memphis offers convenient corporate locations
  • Music Pilgrimage: Stay downtown for Beale Street or near Sun Studio
  • Pet Owners: Many hotels charge pet fees - check policies in advance
  • Large Groups: Consider hotels near the Cook Convention Center

8. Tips for Finding the Best Deals

Money-Saving Strategies

  • Book directly with hotels for potential discounts
  • Check for package deals that include attraction tickets
  • Sign up for hotel loyalty programs
  • Consider Sunday-Thursday stays for lower rates
  • Look for hotels just outside downtown for better value
  • Check Groupon or other deal sites for special offers
