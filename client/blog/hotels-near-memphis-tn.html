The Ultimate Guide to Hotels in Memphis, Tennessee

Memphis, the birthplace of blues and the home of Graceland, offers visitors a rich cultural experience with its music history, delicious barbecue, and Southern hospitality. Choosing the right hotel can make your visit even more memorable. This comprehensive guide covers everything you need to know about hotels in Memphis.

1. Popular Hotel Districts and Neighborhoods

Memphis has several distinct areas where hotels are concentrated, each offering different advantages:

  • Downtown Memphis: The heart of the city with easy access to Beale Street, the Mississippi River, and major attractions. Popular with tourists.
  • Midtown: A more residential area with boutique hotels, close to Overton Park and the Memphis Zoo.
  • East Memphis: Business-friendly area with many chain hotels, near shopping and dining options.
  • Graceland Area: Hotels near Elvis Presley's famous home, convenient for fans but further from downtown.
  • Medical District: Near hospitals and medical centers, often offering competitive rates.

2. Average Hotel Prices and Seasonal Variations

Hotel prices in Memphis vary significantly by season and location:

  • Budget hotels: $70-$120 per night
  • Mid-range hotels: $120-$250 per night
  • Luxury hotels: $250-$500+ per night

Seasonal variations:

  • Peak season (April-June, September-October): Higher prices due to pleasant weather and festivals
  • Summer (July-August): Hot weather but slightly lower prices
  • Winter (November-March): Lowest prices except during holiday periods
  • Special events: Prices spike during Elvis Week (August), Memphis in May festival, and major conventions

4. Nearby Attractions and Points of Interest

Memphis is packed with attractions that influence hotel choices:

  • Beale Street: The famous entertainment district with blues clubs and restaurants
  • Graceland: Elvis Presley's mansion and museum complex
  • National Civil Rights Museum: At the Lorraine Motel where Dr. Martin Luther King Jr. was assassinated
  • Sun Studio: Legendary recording studio where Elvis, Johnny Cash and others recorded
  • Memphis Zoo: One of the top zoos in the U.S.
  • Mississippi Riverfront: Riverboat cruises and parks
  • Bass Pro Shops Pyramid: Massive sporting goods store in a pyramid with observation deck

5. Transportation Options and Accessibility

Getting around Memphis from your hotel:

  • Memphis International Airport (MEM): About 15 minutes from downtown
  • Public Transit (MATA): Buses and trolleys serve major areas
  • Ride-sharing: Uber and Lyft widely available
  • Car Rentals: Available at airport and downtown
  • Walking: Downtown is walkable, other areas less so
  • Biking: Bike share program available
  • Accessibility: Most hotels comply with ADA requirements - confirm specific needs when booking

6. Best Times to Visit

Consider these factors when planning your Memphis hotel stay:

  • Weather: Spring (April-May) and fall (September-October) offer the most pleasant temperatures
  • Festivals: Memphis in May (music festival), Elvis Week in August, BBQ contests
  • Crowds: Summer sees more families, spring/fall more adult tourists
  • Hotel availability: Weekends often book up, especially during events
  • Value: January-February (except MLK Day weekend) offers the best hotel deals

7. Special Considerations for Travelers

  • Families: Look for hotels with pools and near the zoo or children's museum
  • Business travelers: East Memphis has many business hotels with meeting spaces
  • Music fans: Stay downtown for Beale Street or near Graceland for Elvis sites
  • Foodies: Downtown and Midtown offer the best dining options
  • Accessibility needs: Many hotels offer accessible rooms - confirm specifics when booking
  • Pet owners: Several hotels are pet-friendly, often with fees

Pro Tip:

If visiting Graceland is your main purpose, consider staying in that area rather than downtown to save time commuting. However, you'll have fewer dining and entertainment options in the evening.
