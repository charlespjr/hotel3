A Comprehensive Guide to Hotels in New Orleans, LA

New Orleans, the birthplace of jazz and home to mouthwatering Creole cuisine, offers visitors a unique blend of history, culture, and excitement. Choosing the right hotel can make or break your NOLA experience. This guide covers everything you need to know about hotels in The Big Easy.

1. Popular Hotel Districts and Neighborhoods

New Orleans has several distinct areas where hotels are concentrated, each offering a different vibe:

  • French Quarter: The heart of tourist activity with historic charm, nightlife, and easy access to major attractions.
  • Central Business District (CBD)/Warehouse District: More modern hotels, close to the convention center and arts district.
  • Garden District: Upscale area with beautiful mansions and boutique hotels, accessible via streetcar.
  • Faubourg Marigny/Bywater: Bohemian neighborhoods with quirky B&Bs, popular with younger travelers.
  • Mid-City: More residential feel, near City Park and the Museum of Art.

2. Average Hotel Prices and Seasonal Variations

Hotel prices in New Orleans fluctuate significantly based on season and events:

  • Peak Season (Mardi Gras, Jazz Fest, Halloween, Sugar Bowl): $300-$600/night for mid-range hotels, with minimum stay requirements.
  • High Season (October-November, March-May): $200-$400/night for mid-range hotels.
  • Shoulder Season (June, September): $150-$250/night.
  • Low Season (July-August, December after holidays): $100-$200/night, with potential for great deals.
Tip: Book at least 6 months in advance for Mardi Gras or Jazz Fest, as hotels sell out quickly and prices skyrocket closer to the dates.

3. Types of Hotels Available

New Orleans offers accommodation options for every taste and budget:

  • Luxury Hotels: The Roosevelt, Ritz-Carlton, Four Seasons, Windsor Court
  • Historic Hotels: Hotel Monteleone, Omni Royal Orleans, Bienville House
  • Boutique Hotels: Ace Hotel, The Eliza Jane, Hotel Peter and Paul
  • Budget Hotels: HI New Orleans Hostel, La Quinta Inn, Holiday Inn Express
  • B&Bs and Guesthouses: Numerous options in the Garden District and Marigny
  • Chain Hotels: Hilton, Marriott, and Hyatt properties throughout the CBD

4. Nearby Attractions and Points of Interest

Most hotels are conveniently located near major attractions:

  • French Quarter: Bourbon Street, Jackson Square, French Market, St. Louis Cathedral
  • CBD/Warehouse District: National WWII Museum, Contemporary Arts Center, Julia Street galleries
  • Garden District: Magazine Street shopping, Lafayette Cemetery, Commander's Palace
  • Citywide: Audubon Zoo, City Park, Steamboat Natchez cruises

5. Transportation Options and Accessibility

Getting around New Orleans from your hotel is relatively easy:

  • Streetcar: The St. Charles line connects the CBD to the Garden District and Uptown ($1.25 per ride).
  • Buses: Extensive network covering most areas of the city.
  • Biking: Blue Bikes available for rent throughout the city.
  • Walking: The French Quarter and CBD are very walkable.
  • Taxis/Rideshare: Uber and Lyft widely available.
  • Airport: Louis Armstrong International (MSY) is about 15 miles west of downtown.

6. Best Times to Visit

Consider these factors when planning your trip:

  • Weather: Spring (March-May) and fall (October-November) offer pleasant temperatures.
  • Events: Mardi Gras (Feb/March) and Jazz Fest (late April/early May) are spectacular but crowded.
  • Value: Summer (except 4th of July) and December (after holidays) offer lower prices.
  • Avoid: August is extremely hot and humid with higher chance of hurricanes.

7. Special Considerations for Travelers

  • Accessibility: Many historic hotels have limited accessibility - check before booking if mobility is a concern.
  • Noise: French Quarter hotels can be loud, especially on weekends - request a quiet room if needed.
  • Parking: Many hotels charge $30-$50/night for parking - factor this into your budget.
  • Pets: Some hotels are pet-friendly, often with additional fees.

8. Tips for Finding the Best Deals

  • Book directly with hotels for potential perks like room upgrades or dining credits.
  • Consider package deals that include airfare and hotel.
  • Check for government or AAA rates if applicable.
  • Look for last-minute deals during slow periods.
  • Consider staying just outside the French Quarter for better value.
  • Follow hotels on social media for flash sales.

9. Local Amenities and Services

Most hotels offer or are near:

  • 24-hour concierge services (especially in luxury hotels)
  • On-site restaurants featuring local cuisine
  • Swimming pools (a welcome relief in summer)
  • Fitness centers
  • Business centers
  • Nearby pharmacies, grocery stores, and laundromats

10. Safety and Security Information

While New Orleans is generally safe for tourists, keep these tips in mind:

  • Stick to well-lit, populated areas at night.
  • Don't wander alone in unfamiliar neighborhoods after dark.
  • Use hotel safes for valuables.
  • Be cautious with street parking - use secure hotel parking when possible.
  • During Mardi Gras, be extra vigilant with personal belongings in crowds.
  • Most hotels have 24-hour security, especially in the French Quarter.
Final Tip: Wherever you stay in New Orleans, allow time to simply wander and discover the city's unique charm - some of the best experiences happen when you step off the beaten path (while staying in safe areas).
