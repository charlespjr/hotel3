The Ultimate Guide to Hotels in El Paso, Texas

Nestled along the Rio Grande and bordering Mexico, El Paso offers a unique blend of Southwestern charm, rich cultural heritage, and stunning desert landscapes. Whether you're visiting for business or pleasure, finding the right hotel is key to enjoying your stay in this vibrant border city.

1. Popular Hotel Districts and Neighborhoods

El Paso's hotels are concentrated in several key areas, each with its own advantages:

  • Downtown El Paso: The heart of the city with historic buildings, close to government offices and entertainment venues.
  • West Side (Near UTEP): Upscale area near the University of Texas at El Paso with scenic mountain views.
  • East Side: Fast-growing suburban area with newer hotels and shopping centers.
  • Airport Area: Convenient for business travelers and those with early flights.
  • Sunland Park: Near the New Mexico border with casino resorts and shopping.

2. Average Hotel Prices and Seasonal Variations

Hotel rates in El Paso are generally affordable compared to other major Texas cities:

  • Budget hotels: $60-$90 per night
  • Mid-range hotels: $90-$150 per night
  • Luxury hotels: $150-$300+ per night

Seasonal variations:

  • Peak seasons: Spring (March-May) and Fall (September-November) when temperatures are mild
  • Shoulder seasons: Summer (hot but lower prices) and winter holidays (moderate prices)
  • Special events: Prices may spike during major events like the Sun Bowl or Mexican Independence Day celebrations

3. Types of Hotels Available

El Paso offers accommodation options for every taste and budget:

  • Luxury Hotels: The Plaza Hotel Pioneer Park, Hotel Indigo, Stanton House
  • Budget Options: Red Roof Inn, Motel 6, various chain hotels
  • Boutique Hotels: Hotel Paso del Norte, Gardner Hotel (historic)
  • Extended Stay: Homewood Suites, Candlewood Suites
  • Resorts: Sunland Park Racetrack & Casino (just across the NM border)

4. Nearby Attractions and Points of Interest

Many hotels are conveniently located near El Paso's top attractions:

  • Franklin Mountains State Park: Hiking and outdoor activities
  • El Paso Museum of Art: Impressive art collection
  • Plaza Theatre: Historic performing arts venue
  • Wyler Aerial Tramway: Stunning city views
  • Chamizal National Memorial: Cultural park celebrating U.S.-Mexico relations
  • Ciudad Juárez: Easily accessible Mexican city (check travel advisories)

5. Transportation Options and Accessibility

Getting around El Paso:

  • El Paso International Airport (ELP): 10-15 minutes from most hotels
  • Sun Metro: City bus system with routes near major hotels
  • Ride-sharing: Uber and Lyft widely available
  • Car rentals: Available at airport and throughout city
  • Walking: Downtown area is pedestrian-friendly

6. Best Times to Visit

Ideal visiting periods:

  • Weather-wise: Spring (March-May) and Fall (September-November)
  • Festivals: Consider visiting during the Sun City Music Festival (September) or Plaza Classic Film Festival (August)
  • Budget travelers: Summer offers lower prices but prepare for heat (often 100°F+)

7. Special Considerations for Travelers

  • International visitors: Many hotels cater to Mexican nationals with Spanish-speaking staff
  • Business travelers: Look for hotels with meeting spaces in downtown or near the airport
  • Pet owners: Several pet-friendly hotels available (usually with fees)
  • Accessibility: Most major hotels comply with ADA requirements

Border Crossing Tip

If planning to visit Juárez, Mexico, check current border crossing requirements and safety conditions. Many El Paso hotels offer views of both cities.

8. Tips for Finding the Best Deals

  • Book directly with hotels for potential discounts
  • Check for government/military rates if applicable
  • Consider package deals that include rental cars
  • Look for last-minute deals on hotel apps
  • Join hotel loyalty programs for perks
  • Weekday rates are often cheaper than weekends

9. Local Amenities and Services

What to expect at El Paso hotels:

  • Dining: Many hotels offer Mexican/Southwestern cuisine options
  • WiFi: Generally free at most hotels
  • Parking: Often free at suburban hotels; downtown may charge
  • Business centers: Available at most mid-range and up hotels
  • Pools: Common due to warm climate

10. Safety and Security Information

  • El Paso consistently ranks among the safest large U.S. cities
  • Standard hotel security measures are in place at most properties
  • Downtown is generally safe but be aware of surroundings at night
  • Hotel staff can advise on safe areas to visit
  • As with any city, secure valuables in hotel safes

Final Recommendation

For first-time visitors, we recommend staying downtown or on the west side to be near major attractions. Business travelers may prefer the airport area or downtown for convenience. Whatever your needs, El Paso's hospitality industry offers comfortable accommodations to make your visit enjoyable
