The Ultimate Guide to Hotels in Denver, Colorado

Denver, the Mile High City, offers visitors a vibrant mix of urban sophistication and outdoor adventure. Whether you're visiting for business, leisure, or a bit of both, choosing the right hotel can make all the difference in your experience. This comprehensive guide covers everything you need to know about Denver's hotel scene.

1. Popular Hotel Districts and Neighborhoods

Denver's diverse neighborhoods each offer unique lodging experiences:

  • Downtown/Central Business District: The heart of Denver with high-rise hotels, close to convention centers and business hubs.
  • LoDo (Lower Downtown): Historic district with boutique hotels, near Coors Field and Union Station.
  • Cherry Creek: Upscale shopping district with luxury accommodations.
  • Highlands: Trendy area with boutique options and great dining.
  • RiNo (River North Art District): Artsy neighborhood with hip hotels and breweries.
  • Airport Area: Convenient for early flights or short stays, with budget-friendly options.
  • Tech Center: Southeast Denver business district with corporate hotels.

2. Average Hotel Prices and Seasonal Variations

Denver hotel prices fluctuate significantly by season:

  • Budget hotels: $80-$150 per night
  • Mid-range hotels: $150-$300 per night
  • Luxury hotels: $300-$600+ per night

Peak seasons (highest prices):

  • Summer (June-August) - outdoor activities and festivals
  • December - holiday season and ski traffic
  • During major conventions or sports events

Shoulder seasons (good deals):

  • Spring (April-May)
  • Fall (September-October)

Low season (best prices):

  • Winter (January-March, excluding holidays)

4. Nearby Attractions and Points of Interest

Top attractions near Denver hotels:

  • Downtown/LoDo: Union Station, Larimer Square, 16th Street Mall, Coors Field
  • Arts & Culture: Denver Art Museum, Clyfford Still Museum, Museum of Contemporary Art
  • Outdoors: Denver Botanic Gardens, City Park, Confluence Park
  • Entertainment: Denver Performing Arts Complex, Red Rocks Amphitheatre (30 min drive)
  • Day Trips: Rocky Mountain National Park (1.5 hours), Boulder (40 min), Golden (30 min)

5. Transportation Options and Accessibility

Getting around Denver from your hotel:

  • RTD Light Rail: Connects downtown, Tech Center, and Denver International Airport (DIA)
  • Free MallRide: Runs along 16th Street Mall downtown
  • Bike Share: Denver B-cycle stations throughout city
  • Ride Share: Uber and Lyft widely available
  • Car Rentals: Available downtown and at DIA
  • Accessibility: Most hotels comply with ADA requirements; confirm specific needs when booking

6. Best Times to Visit

Ideal times depend on your priorities:

  • For pleasant weather: May-June or September-October (mild temperatures, less rain)
  • For festivals: Summer (Great American Beer Festival, etc.)
  • For skiing: December-March (though ski resorts are 1-2 hours away)
  • For deals: January-February (excluding holiday weekends)

7. Special Considerations for Travelers

  • Altitude: Denver is at 5,280 feet - stay hydrated and take it easy the first day
    • Business travelers: Many downtown hotels offer business centers and meeting spaces
  • Families: Look for hotels with pools and suites; some offer kids-stay-free policies
  • Pet owners: Many Denver hotels are pet-friendly (often with fees)
  • LGBTQ+ travelers: Denver is very welcoming; Capitol Hill has LGBTQ+-friendly accommodations

8. Tips for Finding the Best Deals

Money-Saving Strategies:

  • Book mid-week for better rates (Tuesday-Thursday often cheapest)
  • Check hotel websites for "stay 3 nights, get 4th free" deals
  • Look for packages that include parking (valuable in downtown)
  • Sign up for hotel loyalty programs for member discounts
