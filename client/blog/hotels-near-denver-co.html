The Ultimate Guide to Hotels in Denver, CO

Denver, the Mile High City, offers visitors a vibrant mix of urban sophistication and outdoor adventure. Whether you're visiting for business, pleasure, or a bit of both, choosing the right hotel can make all the difference in your experience. This comprehensive guide covers everything you need to know about Denver hotels.

1. Popular Hotel Districts and Neighborhoods

Denver has several distinct areas where hotels are concentrated, each offering a different vibe and proximity to attractions:

  • Downtown/Central Business District - The heart of Denver with luxury hotels and easy access to business centers
  • LoDo (Lower Downtown) - Historic district with boutique hotels near Coors Field and Union Station
  • Cherry Creek - Upscale shopping district with luxury accommodations
  • Highlands - Trendy neighborhood with boutique hotels and great dining options
  • RiNo (River North Art District) - Artsy area with hip hotels and breweries
  • Airport Area - Convenient for early flights or short stays
  • Tech Center - Business-oriented hotels in Denver's southern suburbs

2. Average Hotel Prices and Seasonal Variations

Denver hotel prices fluctuate significantly based on season and events:

  • Budget hotels: $80-$150 per night
  • Mid-range hotels: $150-$300 per night
  • Luxury hotels: $300-$600+ per night

Seasonal variations:

  • Peak seasons: June-August (summer), December (ski season starts)
  • Shoulder seasons: April-May, September-October
  • Low season: November (except Thanksgiving), January-February (except holidays)

Prices can spike during major events like the Great American Beer Festival, Denver Broncos home games, or the National Western Stock Show.

3. Types of Hotels Available

Denver offers accommodations for every taste and budget:

  • Luxury Hotels: The Ritz-Carlton, Four Seasons, Brown Palace Hotel
  • Boutique Hotels: The Crawford Hotel, The Maven Hotel, The ART Hotel
  • Budget Hotels: Motel 6, Super 8, various independent motels
  • Extended Stay: Residence Inn, Homewood Suites, Extended Stay America
  • Historic Hotels: Oxford Hotel, The Brown Palace
  • Eco-Friendly Hotels: The Rally Hotel, The Source Hotel

4. Nearby Attractions and Points of Interest

Many Denver hotels are conveniently located near top attractions:

  • Downtown: 16th Street Mall, Denver Art Museum, Colorado State Capitol
  • LoDo: Union Station, Coors Field, Larimer Square
  • Golden Triangle: Denver Art Museum, Clyfford Still Museum
  • City Park: Denver Zoo, Denver Museum of Nature & Science
  • Highlands: Local boutiques, restaurants, and views of downtown

Most hotels are within 30-90 minutes of Rocky Mountain National Park and major ski resorts.

5. Transportation Options and Accessibility

Getting around Denver from your hotel is relatively easy:

  • Public Transit: RTD buses and light rail serve most areas
  • Free MallRide: Runs along 16th Street Mall downtown
  • Bike Share: Denver B-cycle stations throughout the city
  • Ride Share: Uber and Lyft widely available
  • Airport Transit: A Line train connects DEN to Union Station in 37 minutes
  • Accessibility: Most hotels comply with ADA requirements

6. Best Times to Visit

The ideal time to visit Denver depends on your priorities:

  • For pleasant weather: May-June or September-October
  • For skiing: December-March (though mountains are best January-February)
  • For festivals: Summer months feature numerous events
  • For lower prices: Late April or November (avoid holidays)

7. Special Considerations for Travelers

  • Altitude: Denver is at 5,280 feet - stay hydrated and take it easy at first
  • Weather: Pack layers as temperatures can vary dramatically
  • Parking: Many downtown hotels charge $30-$50/night for parking
  • Pet-friendly: Many hotels accommodate pets (often with fees)
  • Business travelers: Look for hotels with good workspaces and meeting rooms

8. Tips for Finding the Best Deals

Follow these strategies to save on Denver hotels:

  • Book 3-6 weeks in advance for best rates
  • Check hotel websites for exclusive deals
  • Consider package deals with flights or rental cars
  • Look for hotels slightly outside downtown for better value
  • Join hotel loyalty programs for member rates
  • Travel mid-week when business travelers leave
  • Use price comparison tools but verify directly with hotels

9. Local Amenities and Services

Most Denver hotels offer these amenities and services:

  • 24-hour front desk service
  • Business centers and meeting rooms
  • Fitness centers (often with altitude training equipment)
  • On-site dining options
  • Concierge services for local recommendations
  • Many have rooftop bars with mountain views
  • Increasing number with EV charging stations
