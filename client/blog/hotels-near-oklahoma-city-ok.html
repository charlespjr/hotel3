Your Complete Guide to Hotels in Oklahoma City, OK

Oklahoma City (OKC) offers visitors a blend of Western heritage, modern attractions, and Southern hospitality. Whether you're visiting for business or pleasure, finding the right hotel can make your stay more enjoyable. This comprehensive guide covers everything you need to know about hotels in Oklahoma City.

1. Popular Hotel Districts and Neighborhoods

Oklahoma City has several distinct areas where hotels are concentrated, each offering different advantages:

  • Bricktown: The city's entertainment district with nightlife, restaurants, and attractions like the Bricktown Canal.
  • Downtown: Close to business centers, the Oklahoma City National Memorial, and the Arts District.
  • Midtown: A revitalized area with trendy restaurants and boutique hotels.
  • Northwest Expressway: Convenient to the airport with many chain hotels at moderate prices.
  • Meridian Avenue: Near shopping and restaurants, with a mix of budget and mid-range options.
  • Near Adventure District: Close to the Oklahoma City Zoo and Science Museum Oklahoma.

2. Average Hotel Prices and Seasonal Variations

Hotel prices in Oklahoma City are generally affordable compared to other major U.S. cities:

  • Budget hotels: $60-$100 per night
  • Mid-range hotels: $100-$180 per night
  • Luxury hotels: $200-$400 per night
  • Boutique hotels: $150-$300 per night

Prices fluctuate based on:

  • Peak seasons: Spring (March-May) and Fall (September-November) when weather is mild and events are plentiful
  • High-demand periods: During major events like the Oklahoma State Fair (September), NBA Thunder games, and college sports tournaments
  • Low season: Summer (June-August) can be hot, and winter (December-February) sees fewer tourists, leading to better deals

3. Types of Hotels Available

Oklahoma City offers accommodations for every taste and budget:

  • Luxury Hotels:
    • The Skirvin Hilton Oklahoma City (historic downtown hotel)
    • 21c Museum Hotel Oklahoma City (art-focused boutique luxury)
    • Colcord Hotel (elegant downtown option)
  • Budget Hotels:
    • Various Motel 6, Super 8, and Econo Lodge locations
    • Microtel Inn & Suites by Wyndham
  • Boutique Hotels:
    • The Bradford House (charming midtown option)
    • The Ellison Hotel (upscale boutique in Midtown)
  • Extended Stay Hotels:
    • Homewood Suites by Hilton
    • Staybridge Suites
    • Candlewood Suites
  • Business Hotels:
    • Renaissance Oklahoma City Convention Center Hotel
    • Sheraton Oklahoma City Downtown Hotel

4. Nearby Attractions and Points of Interest

Many hotels are conveniently located near major attractions:

  • Oklahoma City National Memorial & Museum: Commemorating the 1995 bombing
  • Bricktown Entertainment District: Restaurants, clubs, and the Bricktown Canal
  • Myriad Botanical Gardens: Beautiful outdoor space with Crystal Bridge Tropical Conservatory
  • Oklahoma City Museum of Art: Featuring an impressive Chihuly glass collection
  • Scissortail Park: 70-acre urban park with regular events
  • Adventure District: Includes the Oklahoma City Zoo, Science Museum Oklahoma, and Frontier City amusement park
  • Cowboy & Western Heritage Museum: Celebrating Oklahoma's Western history

5. Transportation Options and Accessibility

Getting around Oklahoma City:

  • Will Rogers World Airport (OKC): About 8 miles southwest of downtown with many airport hotels offering shuttles
  • Public Transit: EMBARK bus system serves most areas, with streetcars connecting downtown, Bricktown, and Midtown
  • Ride-sharing: Uber and Lyft operate throughout the city
  • Car Rentals: Available at the airport and throughout the city
  • Biking: Spokies bike share program with stations around downtown
  • Accessibility: Most hotels comply with ADA requirements; confirm specific needs when booking

6. Best Times to Visit

Consider these factors when planning your trip:

  • Weather: Spring (April-May) and fall (September-October) offer mild temperatures
  • Events: Festival season peaks in spring and fall with events like the Festival of the Arts (April) and Oklahoma State Fair (September)
  • Lower prices: Summer and winter often have hotel deals, though summer can be very hot
  • Sports seasons: NBA Thunder season runs October-April; college football is big in the fall

7. Special Considerations for Travelers

  • Business travelers: Downtown hotels offer proximity to the business district and convention center
  • Families: Look for hotels near the Adventure District with pools and free breakfast
  • Pet owners: Many hotels are pet-friendly; check policies in advance
  • Accessibility needs: Most hotels offer ADA-compliant rooms; confirm specific requirements when booking
  • Large groups: Consider hotels near the Cox Convention Center or with meeting spaces

Pro Tip:

If you're visiting for a Thunder game or concert at the Paycom Center, book early as downtown hotels fill quickly for these events.
