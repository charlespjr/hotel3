Your Complete Guide to Hotels in Tulsa, Oklahoma

Tulsa, Oklahoma's second-largest city, offers visitors a unique blend of art deco architecture, rich history, and modern amenities. Whether you're visiting for business or pleasure, finding the right hotel can make all the difference in your experience. This comprehensive guide covers everything you need to know about staying in Tulsa.

1. Popular Hotel Districts and Neighborhoods

Tulsa has several distinct areas where hotels are concentrated, each offering different advantages:

Downtown Tulsa

The heart of the city with upscale hotels, close to major attractions like the BOK Center and Cain's Ballroom. Ideal for business travelers and those wanting to be in the center of the action.

Blue Dome District

Known for its vibrant nightlife and historic buildings, this area features boutique hotels and is popular with younger travelers.

Brookside

A trendy neighborhood with charming bed-and-breakfast options and boutique hotels, surrounded by excellent dining and shopping.

Near Woodland Hills Mall

This area offers many mid-range chain hotels convenient for shoppers and those wanting easy highway access.

Airport Area

Several budget and mid-range hotels cluster around Tulsa International Airport, ideal for early flights or short stays.

2. Average Hotel Prices and Seasonal Variations

Hotel prices in Tulsa vary significantly by season and location:

  • Budget hotels: $60-$100 per night
  • Mid-range hotels: $100-$180 per night
  • Luxury hotels: $200-$400 per night
  • Boutique hotels/B&Bs: $150-$300 per night

Seasonal variations:

  • Peak season (April-June, September-October): Prices are highest during these pleasant weather months and during major events.
  • Summer (July-August): Slightly lower prices despite the heat, except during special events.
  • Winter (November-March): Lowest prices, except around holidays.

4. Nearby Attractions and Points of Interest

Many Tulsa hotels are conveniently located near major attractions:

  • Philbrook Museum of Art: Beautiful villa-turned-museum with impressive gardens
  • Gathering Place: Award-winning 100-acre riverfront park
  • Woody Guthrie Center: Museum dedicated to the folk music legend
  • Cain's Ballroom: Historic music venue famous for country and rock performances
  • Route 66 attractions: Including the Blue Whale of Catoosa and numerous historic sites
  • BOK Center: Major arena hosting concerts and sporting events

5. Transportation Options and Accessibility

Getting Around Tulsa

  • Car: Most convenient option as Tulsa is spread out. Hotels typically offer parking (sometimes for a fee).
  • Public Transit: Tulsa Transit buses serve major areas but may be limited for tourists.
  • Rideshare: Uber and Lyft operate throughout the city.
  • Taxis: Available but less common than rideshares.
  • Biking: Some hotels offer bike rentals, and downtown has bike lanes.

Accessibility

Most major hotels in Tulsa comply with ADA requirements, offering:

  • Wheelchair-accessible rooms
  • Roll-in showers
  • Visual alarms
  • Accessible public spaces

Always confirm specific accessibility needs when booking.

6. Best Times to Visit

The ideal time to visit Tulsa depends on your preferences:

  • Spring (April-May): Pleasant temperatures, blooming gardens, and outdoor festivals.
  • Fall (September-October): Comfortable weather, fall foliage, and cultural events.
  • Summer (June-August): Hot and humid but offers summer concerts and events.
  • Winter (December): Holiday lights and decorations, fewer crowds.

Consider avoiding March if you're allergic to pollen, as this is peak allergy season.

7. Special Considerations for Travelers

Business Travelers

  • Downtown hotels offer best access to business centers
  • Many hotels have business centers and meeting rooms
  • Weekday rates may be higher than weekends

Families

  • Look for hotels with pools and free breakfast
  • Suites with kitchenettes can be cost-effective
  • Check age policies for children staying free

Pet Owners

  • Many hotels allow pets (usually with fee and restrictions)
  • Some boutique hotels are more pet-friendly than chains
  • River Parks offer great pet walking areas
