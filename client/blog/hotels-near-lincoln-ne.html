Hotels in Lincoln, NE – A Complete Travel Guide

Lincoln, Nebraska, is a vibrant city known for its rich history, college-town energy (thanks to the University of Nebraska-Lincoln), and Midwestern hospitality. Whether you're visiting for business, a football game, or a family vacation, finding the right hotel is key to a great trip. This guide covers everything you need to know about hotels in Lincoln.

1. Popular Hotel Districts & Neighborhoods

Lincoln's hotels are concentrated in a few key areas:

  • Downtown/Haymarket District – Best for nightlife, dining, and events. Close to Pinnacle Bank Arena.
  • Near South (UNL Campus Area) – Ideal for university visitors and game days.
  • Airport Area (NW 48th St) – Convenient for business travelers and road-trippers.
  • East O Street Corridor – Budget-friendly options near major highways.
  • Pioneers Park Area (Southwest Lincoln) – Quieter, suburban feel with mid-range hotels.

2. Average Hotel Prices & Seasonal Variations

Lincoln's hotel prices fluctuate based on events and seasons:

  • Budget hotels/motels: $60–$100 per night
  • Mid-range hotels: $100–$180 per night
  • Upscale/luxury hotels: $180–$300+ per night

Peak Seasons: Prices spike during:

  • University of Nebraska football home games (Fall weekends)
  • Graduation weekends (May & December)
  • State Fair (late August–early September)

Best Value: Winter (January–February) and weekdays generally offer lower rates.

3. Types of Hotels Available

Lincoln offers accommodations for every preference and budget:

  • Luxury: The Graduate Lincoln (boutique), Embassy Suites Downtown
  • Boutique: The Kindler Hotel (historic charm), The Lincoln Marriott Cornhusker
  • Budget: Motel 6, Super 8, Econo Lodge
  • Extended Stay: Candlewood Suites, Homewood Suites
  • Business-Friendly: Hyatt Place, Hilton Garden Inn

4. Nearby Attractions & Points of Interest

Top attractions near Lincoln hotels:

  • Downtown/Haymarket: Pinnacle Bank Arena, Lincoln Children's Museum, Historic Haymarket shops
  • UNL Area: Memorial Stadium, Sheldon Museum of Art, International Quilt Museum
  • Citywide: Sunken Gardens, Nebraska State Capitol, Pioneers Park Nature Center

5. Transportation Options & Accessibility

  • Airport: Lincoln Airport (LNK) has limited flights; many visitors fly into Omaha (OMA) and drive ~1 hour.
  • Public Transit: StarTran buses serve major areas ($1.75/ride).
  • Ride Shares: Uber & Lyft operate in Lincoln.
  • Parking: Downtown hotels often charge $10–$20/night for parking.
  • Walkability: Downtown/Haymarket is very walkable; other areas require a car.

6. Best Times to Visit

  • For Events: Fall (football season), May (graduation/gardens in bloom)
  • For Good Weather: April–June & September–October
  • For Budget Travel: January–February (avoid game weekends)

7. Special Considerations for Travelers

  • Business Travelers: Downtown hotels offer best access to government/corporate offices.
  • Families: Look for hotels with pools; many offer free stays for kids.
  • Disabled Access: Most major hotels comply with ADA; confirm specific needs when booking.
  • Pet Owners: Several hotels (like Staybridge Suites) allow pets for a fee.

8. Tips for Finding the Best Deals

  • Book directly through hotel websites for potential member discounts.
  • Check for "government rates" if eligible (many hotels offer them).
  • Use price comparison tools like Google Hotels or Kayak.
  • Consider package deals if visiting for events (hotel + tickets).
  • Sign up for loyalty programs (Hilton Honors, Marriott Bonvoy, etc.).

9. Local Amenities & Services

What to expect from Lincoln hotels:

  • Free Breakfast: Common at mid-range hotels (Hampton Inn, Holiday Inn Express).
  • Gyms/Pools: Most hotels have fitness centers; pools are seasonal at many properties.
  • Dining: Downtown hotels often have restaurants; airport-area hotels may lack on-site options.
  • 24/7 Front Desk: Standard at major chains; some budget motels may have limited hours.

10. Safety & Security Information

  • Lincoln is generally safe, but take normal urban precautions.
  • Downtown is well-lit and patrolled, especially near entertainment venues.
  • Most hotels have electronic key cards and security cameras.
  • Avoid leaving valuables in parked cars, especially near stadiums on game days.
  • For emergencies, dial 911. Non-emergency police: (402) 441-6000.

Final Tip

Wherever you stay in Lincoln, you're rarely more than a 15-minute drive from major attractions. Prioritize location based on your itinerary—being close to your primary destinations will maximize your enjoyment of this friendly capital city.
