Hotels in Long Beach, CA: A Complete Travel Guide

Long Beach, California, is a vibrant coastal city known for its waterfront attractions, cultural diversity, and thriving arts scene. Whether you're visiting for business or leisure, choosing the right hotel can make your stay unforgettable. This guide covers everything you need to know about hotels in Long Beach, from pricing to nearby attractions.

1. Popular Hotel Districts and Neighborhoods

Long Beach offers several key areas with distinct vibes and accommodations:

  • Downtown Long Beach: The heart of the city, featuring upscale hotels, dining, and nightlife.
  • The Waterfront: Home to luxury resorts with ocean views and proximity to the Queen Mary.
  • Belmont Shore: A relaxed beachside neighborhood with boutique hotels and vacation rentals.
  • Signal Hill: Offers budget-friendly options with easy freeway access.
  • Bixby Knolls: A quieter area with mid-range hotels, ideal for families.

2. Average Hotel Prices and Seasonal Variations

Hotel rates in Long Beach vary by season and location:

  • Budget hotels: $80–$150 per night
  • Mid-range hotels: $150–$250 per night
  • Luxury hotels/resorts: $250–$500+ per night

Peak season (June–August & holidays): Prices increase by 20–40% due to high demand.
Off-season (January–March): Discounts and deals are more common.

3. Types of Hotels Available

  • Luxury: The Westin Long Beach, Hyatt Centric The Pike
  • Budget: Motel 6, Super 8, Travelodge
  • Boutique: Hotel Maya, The Varden Hotel
  • Extended Stay: Residence Inn by Marriott, Homewood Suites
  • Beachfront Resorts: The Queen Mary Hotel, Renaissance Long Beach

4. Nearby Attractions and Points of Interest

  • Queen Mary: A historic ocean liner turned hotel and museum.
  • Aquarium of the Pacific: One of the top aquariums in the U.S.
  • Shoreline Village: A charming waterfront shopping and dining district.
  • Long Beach Convention Center: Hosts major events and conferences.
  • El Dorado Nature Center: A peaceful retreat for nature lovers.

5. Transportation Options and Accessibility

  • Public Transit: Long Beach Transit buses and the Metro Blue Line provide easy access to LA.
  • Bike Rentals: Long Beach is bike-friendly with dedicated lanes.
  • Water Taxis: A fun way to explore the waterfront.
  • Airport Proximity: Long Beach Airport (LGB) is just 10 minutes from downtown.

6. Best Times to Visit

The ideal time to visit depends on your preferences:

  • Summer (June–August): Warm weather, beach activities, and festivals.
  • Spring/Fall: Mild temperatures, fewer crowds, and lower prices.
  • Winter: Cooler but still pleasant, with holiday events.

7. Special Considerations for Travelers

  • Families: Look for hotels with pools and kid-friendly amenities.
  • Business Travelers: Downtown hotels offer proximity to the convention center.
  • Pet Owners: Some hotels, like Hotel Maya, are pet-friendly.

8. Tips for Finding the Best Deals

  • Book mid-week for lower rates.
  • Check hotel websites for exclusive promotions.
  • Use price comparison tools like Kayak or Booking.com.
  • Consider package deals that include attractions.

9. Local Amenities and Services

  • Dining: From seafood at Parker’s Lighthouse to casual eats at The Attic.
  • Shopping: The Pike Outlets and 2nd Street in Belmont Shore.
  • Nightlife: Downtown bars and clubs like The Stave.

10. Safety and Security Information

  • Long Beach is generally safe, but exercise caution in less touristy areas at night.
  • Hotels in well-lit, central areas (Downtown, Waterfront) are the most secure.
  • Use hotel safes for valuables and lock rental cars.

Final Thoughts

Long Beach offers a diverse range of accommodations to suit every traveler’s needs. Whether you're looking for a luxury resort, a budget-friendly stay, or a unique boutique experience, you'll find the perfect hotel in this dynamic coastal city. Plan ahead, take advantage of deals, and enjoy everything Long Beach has to offer!
