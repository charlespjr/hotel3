Hotels in Long Beach, CA: A Comprehensive Guide

Long Beach, California, is a vibrant coastal city known for its waterfront attractions, cultural diversity, and year-round pleasant weather. Whether you're visiting for business or leisure, finding the right hotel can make all the difference in your experience. This guide covers everything you need to know about hotels in Long Beach.

1. Popular Hotel Districts and Neighborhoods

Long Beach offers several distinct areas with hotel options to suit different preferences:

  • Downtown/Waterfront: The heart of Long Beach with luxury hotels and easy access to attractions
  • Belmont Shore: A charming beachside community with boutique options
  • Signal Hill: Offers budget-friendly hotels with great city views
  • Long Beach Airport Area: Convenient for business travelers and early flights
  • Queen Mary/Shoreline Village: Unique historic and waterfront accommodations

2. Average Hotel Prices and Seasonal Variations

Hotel prices in Long Beach vary significantly by season and location:

  • Budget hotels: $80-$150 per night
  • Mid-range hotels: $150-$300 per night
  • Luxury hotels: $300-$600+ per night

Peak seasons (higher prices): Summer (June-August) and during major events like the Grand Prix (April)

Shoulder seasons (better deals): Spring (March-May) and Fall (September-November)

Low season (best prices): Winter (December-February), excluding holidays

3. Types of Hotels Available

Long Beach offers accommodation options for every taste and budget:

  • Luxury Hotels: The Westin Long Beach, Hyatt Regency Long Beach
  • Boutique Hotels: The Varden Hotel, Hotel Maya
  • Budget Hotels: Motel 6, Super 8, Rodeway Inn
  • Extended Stay: Residence Inn, Homewood Suites
  • Unique Stays: The Queen Mary Hotel (historic ocean liner)
  • Beachfront Properties: The Cove Hotel, Hyatt Centric The Pike

4. Nearby Attractions and Points of Interest

Many Long Beach hotels are conveniently located near popular attractions:

  • The Queen Mary
  • Aquarium of the Pacific
  • Shoreline Village
  • Long Beach Convention Center
  • Pine Avenue dining and entertainment district
  • Belmont Pier
  • Museum of Latin American Art
  • El Dorado Nature Center
  • Catalina Island (ferry access)

5. Transportation Options and Accessibility

Getting around Long Beach is relatively easy:

  • Public Transit: Long Beach Transit buses and the free Passport shuttle service
  • Bike Share: Long Beach has an extensive bike share program
  • Water Taxi: Connects waterfront attractions
  • Ride Share: Uber and Lyft widely available
  • Airport: Long Beach Airport (LGB) is just 3 miles from downtown
  • Parking: Most hotels charge for parking ($20-$40/night)

6. Best Times to Visit

The ideal time to visit Long Beach depends on your preferences:

  • For beach weather: June through September
  • For fewer crowds: April-May or September-October
  • For events: Check the calendar for the Long Beach Grand Prix (April), Pride (May), or the Sea Festival (July)
  • For whale watching: December through April

7. Special Considerations for Travelers

Keep these factors in mind when booking your Long Beach stay:

  • Many downtown hotels host business travelers during weekdays and leisure travelers on weekends
  • Some beachfront hotels may have noise from nearby bars and nightlife
  • Accessibility varies - newer hotels generally have better ADA compliance
  • Pet policies differ widely - some hotels charge pet fees or have weight limits
  • Resort fees are common at higher-end properties ($15-$30/night)

8. Tips for Finding the Best Deals

Follow these strategies to save on your Long Beach hotel stay:

  • Book directly with hotels for potential discounts and perks
  • Check for package deals that include parking or attraction tickets
  • Consider last-minute booking apps for same-day discounts
  • Join hotel loyalty programs for member rates
  • Look for hotels slightly outside downtown for better value
  • Travel mid-week for lower rates
  • Check for convention or group rates if applicable

9. Local Amenities and Services

Long Beach hotels offer various amenities and are surrounded by convenient services:

  • Most hotels have pools, with beachfront properties offering ocean views
  • Business centers and meeting spaces are common in downtown hotels
  • Many properties offer bike rentals or beach gear
  • 24-hour fitness centers are standard in mid-range and upscale hotels
  • Nearby services include grocery stores (Vons, Ralphs), pharmacies, and urgent care centers
  • Dining options range from casual beach cafes to upscale waterfront restaurants

10. Safety and Security Information

Long Beach is generally safe, but take these precautions:

  • Downtown and tourist areas are well-patrolled but be aware of your surroundings at night
  • Use hotel safes for valuables
  • Park in well-lit areas or use hotel parking when available
  • Some areas north of downtown are best avoided at night - ask hotel staff for advice
  • Beach areas are safe during the day but may have limited lighting at night
  • Emergency services are responsive - dial 911 if needed
