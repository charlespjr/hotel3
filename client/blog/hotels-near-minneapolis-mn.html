Your Complete Guide to Hotels in Minneapolis, Minnesota

Minneapolis, the larger half of the Twin Cities, offers visitors a vibrant mix of urban sophistication and natural beauty. Whether you're visiting for business, leisure, or a special event, choosing the right hotel can make all the difference in your experience. This comprehensive guide covers everything you need to know about hotels in Minneapolis.

1. Popular Hotel Districts and Neighborhoods

Minneapolis has several distinct areas where hotels are concentrated, each offering its own advantages:

Downtown Minneapolis

The heart of the city with the highest concentration of hotels, including luxury properties and business-oriented accommodations. Perfect for those wanting to be near major attractions and the convention center.

North Loop (Warehouse District)

Trendy neighborhood with boutique hotels, known for its nightlife, upscale dining, and proximity to Target Field (home of the Minnesota Twins).

Uptown

Hip, artsy area with more affordable lodging options near Lake Calhoun and Lake Harriet, popular with younger travelers.

Near the University of Minnesota

Budget-friendly options convenient for campus visitors, with good access to public transportation.

Bloomington (near Mall of America)

About 20 minutes from downtown, this area offers family-friendly hotels with easy access to the massive mall and airport.

Airport Area

Convenient for business travelers or those with early flights, with a range of chain hotels offering shuttle service.

2. Average Hotel Prices and Seasonal Variations

Hotel prices in Minneapolis vary significantly by season, location, and hotel class:

  • Luxury hotels: $250-$400+ per night
  • Mid-range hotels: $150-$250 per night
  • Budget hotels: $80-$150 per night
  • Hostels/Basic motels: $50-$80 per night

Seasonal considerations:

  • Peak seasons: Summer (June-August) and winter holiday season (December) see highest prices
  • Shoulder seasons: Spring (April-May) and fall (September-October) offer moderate prices and pleasant weather
  • Low season: January-March (except for special events) typically has the best deals

Pro Tip: Hotel prices often spike during major events like the Super Bowl (when applicable), the Minnesota State Fair (late August-early September), and Twin Cities Marathon weekend (early October). Book well in advance for these periods.

4. Nearby Attractions and Points of Interest

Choosing a hotel near attractions you plan to visit can save time and transportation costs:

Downtown Area Attractions

  • Nicollet Mall (shopping and dining)
  • Walker Art Center and Minneapolis Sculpture Garden
  • Guthrie Theater (with its famous "Endless Bridge")
  • First Avenue nightclub (music venue made famous by Prince)

Cultural Attractions

  • Minneapolis Institute of Art
  • Mill City Museum (flour milling history)
  • American Swedish Institute

Outdoor Spaces

  • Chain of Lakes (Calhoun, Harriet, Isles)
  • Minnehaha Park and Falls
  • Stone Arch Bridge (great views of downtown)

Sports Venues

  • U.S. Bank Stadium (Vikings football)
  • Target Field (Twins baseball)
  • Target Center (Timberwolves basketball, Lynx WNBA)

Family-Friendly

  • Mall of America (in nearby Bloomington)
  • Minnesota Zoo (in Apple Valley, about 20 miles south)
  • Science Museum of Minnesota (in St. Paul)

5. Transportation Options and Accessibility

Getting around Minneapolis from your hotel is relatively easy with these options:

Public Transportation

  • Light Rail: Metro Blue Line connects downtown to Mall of America and airport; Green Line connects to St. Paul
  • Buses: Extensive network with many downtown hotels near major routes
  • Northstar Commuter Rail: Service to northwestern suburbs

Ride Services

  • Uber and Lyft widely available
  • Traditional taxis (but ride-sharing is often more convenient)
