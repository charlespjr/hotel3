# Are Airbnbs Allowed in NYC? A Complete Guide to Short-Term Rental Regulations New York City is one of the world’s top tourist destinations, but its strict short-term rental laws can make finding an Airbnb confusing. If you're wondering, **"Are Airbnbs allowed in NYC?"**, the answer isn’t straightforward. This guide breaks down NYC’s Airbnb regulations, what’s legal, and how to book safely. ## **Are Airbnbs Legal in NYC?** Yes, but with **strict restrictions**. In 2023, NYC implemented **Local Law 18**, which requires all short-term rental hosts to register with the city. Unregistered listings are illegal, and platforms like Airbnb can’t process bookings for them. ### **Key NYC Airbnb Regulations** - **Registration Required**: Hosts must apply and be approved by the Mayor’s Office of Special Enforcement (OSE). - **No Entire-Apartment Rentals Under 30 Days**: Unless the host is also present (like renting a spare room). - **No Rent-Stabilized or Public Housing Rentals**: These properties cannot be used for short-term stays. - **Two-Guest Maximum**: For stays shorter than 30 days, only two paying guests are allowed per rental. - **Building Restrictions**: Co-ops and condos may have their own rules banning short-term rentals. ## **What Types of Airbnbs Are Allowed in NYC?** ✅ **Legal Options** - **Private Room Rentals**: Hosts can rent out a spare room while they’re home. - **Full-Home Rentals for 30+ Days**: No registration needed for stays longer than 30 days. - **Registered Entire Apartments**: Some licensed hotels and boutique stays operate legally. ❌ **Illegal Options** - Entire apartments rented for less than 30 days (unless registered). - Basement or attic units not meeting safety codes. - Rent-stabilized apartments used for short-term stays. ## **How to Find a Legal Airbnb in NYC** Since many listings disappeared after Local Law 18, here’s how to book safely: ### **1. Check for a Registration Number** - Legal listings should display a **valid registration number** (e.g., "NYC-1234567"). - Avoid listings that don’t show this. ### **2. Look for Licensed Hotels & Boutique Stays** - Some boutique hotels and "Airbnb-friendly" buildings operate legally. - Platforms like **Sonder** and **Blueground** specialize in legal short-term stays. ### **3. Consider Alternatives** - **Extended-Stay Hotels** (e.g., Mint House, The Guild) - **Corporate Housing** (e.g., June Homes, Outsite) - **Sublets Over 30 Days** (Check lease agreements first) ## **Penalties for Breaking NYC Airbnb Laws** Hosts and guests risk fines if caught in illegal rentals: - **Hosts**: Up to **$5,000** for unregistered rentals. - **Guests**: Could be evicted mid-stay if the rental is illegal. - **Booking Platforms**: Fined for processing illegal reservations. ## **Tips for Booking an Airbnb in NYC** ✔ **Verify the listing** – Ask the host for their registration number. ✔ **Read reviews carefully** – Look for mentions of last-minute cancellations (a red flag for illegal listings). ✔ **Book longer stays** – 30+ day rentals avoid registration issues. ✔ **Use trusted platforms** – Some sites vet listings more strictly than others. ## **Final Thoughts: Is Airbnb Still Worth It in NYC?** While legal Airbnbs are harder to find, they still exist—especially for private rooms or long-term stays. For shorter visits, licensed hotels or boutique rentals may be a safer bet. Always double-check listings to avoid scams or illegal setups. ### **Need Help?** - Check NYC’s **[Short-Term Rental Registration Portal](https://www.nyc.gov/site/specialenforcement/short-term-rentals/short-term-rentals.page)** for approved listings. - For legal alternatives, browse **[Furnished Quarters](https://www.furnishedquarters.com/)** or **[Leavetown](https://www.leavetown.com/)**. By understanding NYC’s Airbnb laws, you can book confidently and avoid costly surprises.
