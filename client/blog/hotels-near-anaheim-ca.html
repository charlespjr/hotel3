The Ultimate Guide to Hotels in Anaheim, California

Anaheim, California, is best known as the home of Disneyland Resort, but this vibrant city offers much more for visitors. Whether you're planning a family vacation, business trip, or romantic getaway, choosing the right hotel can make all the difference. This comprehensive guide covers everything you need to know about hotels in Anaheim.

1. Popular Hotel Districts and Neighborhoods

Anaheim has several distinct areas where hotels are concentrated:

  • Disneyland Resort Area - The most popular area with hotels within walking distance to the parks
  • Anaheim Resort District - Surrounds Disneyland with many family-friendly options
  • Garden Grove - Just west of Disneyland with more budget-friendly choices
  • Anaheim Convention Center Area - Ideal for business travelers and convention attendees
  • Downtown Anaheim - Offers a more local experience with boutique options

2. Average Hotel Prices and Seasonal Variations

Hotel prices in Anaheim vary significantly by season and proximity to Disneyland:

  • Budget hotels: $80-$150 per night
  • Mid-range hotels: $150-$300 per night
  • Luxury hotels: $300-$600+ per night

Peak seasons (highest prices):

  • Summer (June-August)
  • Holiday periods (Christmas, New Year's, Spring Break)
  • Major convention dates

Value seasons (lower prices):

  • January (after New Year's) to early March
  • September to mid-December (excluding holidays)
  • Weekdays outside of peak seasons

4. Nearby Attractions and Points of Interest

Beyond Disneyland, Anaheim offers many attractions:

  • Disneyland Resort - The original Disney park and Disney California Adventure
  • Downtown Disney District - Shopping, dining, and entertainment
  • Anaheim Convention Center - One of the largest convention centers on the West Coast
  • Angel Stadium - Home of the Los Angeles Angels baseball team
  • Honda Center - Major venue for concerts and sports
  • Anaheim Packing District - Historic food hall with diverse dining options
  • Knott's Berry Farm - Another popular theme park just 15 minutes away

5. Transportation Options and Accessibility

Getting around Anaheim is relatively easy with these options:

  • Walking: Many hotels near Disneyland are within walking distance
  • Anaheim Resort Transportation (ART): Shuttle service with stops at major hotels and attractions
  • Ride-sharing: Uber and Lyft are widely available
  • Public buses: OCTA operates bus routes throughout the city
  • Metrolink: Regional rail service with an Anaheim station
  • Car rentals: Available at the airport and throughout the city
  • Disneyland Resort Monorail: Connects Downtown Disney with Tomorrowland

For travelers with disabilities, most hotels and attractions offer accessible accommodations. Always check with your specific hotel about accessibility features.

6. Best Times to Visit

The ideal time to visit Anaheim depends on your priorities:

  • For lower crowds: January-February or September-October (weekdays are best)
  • For pleasant weather: April-May or September-October
  • For special events: Check Disneyland's event calendar for seasonal celebrations
  • For convention attendees: Check the Anaheim Convention Center schedule

Pro Tip:

Mid-week visits (Tuesday-Thursday) typically see smaller crowds at Disneyland compared to weekends, regardless of the season.

7. Special Considerations for Travelers

Keep these factors in mind when planning your Anaheim stay:

  • Families: Look for hotels with kid-friendly amenities like pools and free breakfast
  • Business travelers: Choose hotels near the convention center with business centers
  • International visitors: Many hotels have multilingual staff
  • Large groups: Consider hotels with suite options or adjoining rooms
  • Pet owners: Some hotels are pet-friendly (usually with additional fees)
  • Extended stays: Look for hotels with kitchenettes or laundry facilities
