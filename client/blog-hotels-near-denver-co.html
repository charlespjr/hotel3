Hotels Near Denver, CO: Your Complete Guide

June 1, 2024 5 min read

Denver, Colorado's capital and largest city, offers a diverse range of accommodations from luxury downtown hotels to boutique properties in historic neighborhoods. Whether you're visiting for business, exploring the Rocky Mountains, or enjoying the city's vibrant culture, finding the right place to stay in the Mile High City is essential for a great experience. This guide will help you navigate Denver's hotel scene.

Best Areas to Stay in Denver

Denver's neighborhoods each offer unique experiences:

  • LoDo (Lower Downtown): Historic area with luxury hotels
  • RiNo (River North): Artsy area with boutique hotels
  • Cherry Creek: Upscale shopping district with comfortable hotels
  • Highlands: Trendy area with unique stays
  • Union Station: Transportation hub with modern accommodations

Top Hotel Categories in Denver

Luxury Hotels

Experience the height of luxury at properties like The Four Seasons Hotel Denver, The Ritz-Carlton Denver, or The Brown Palace Hotel. These hotels offer world-class amenities, fine dining, and prime locations in Denver's most prestigious areas.

Boutique Hotels

For a more intimate experience, consider boutique hotels in areas like RiNo or Highlands. These properties often feature unique designs and personalized service.

Budget-Friendly Options

Denver offers several affordable hotel chains and independent properties that provide comfortable stays without breaking the bank. Look for deals in areas like the airport or near the convention center.

Tips for Booking Hotels in Denver

  • Book at least 3-4 months in advance for major events
  • Consider altitude when choosing your accommodations
  • Look for hotels with included breakfast (common in Denver)
  • Check for hotel packages that include attraction tickets
  • Read recent reviews to ensure the hotel meets your expectations

Best Time to Visit Denver

The best hotel rates are typically available during:

  • January and February (excluding holiday weekends)
  • Late fall (November)
  • Early spring (March)

Transportation Tips

When choosing your hotel, consider:

  • Proximity to major attractions you plan to visit
  • Access to RTD Light Rail and bus routes
  • Parking availability and costs
  • Distance from Denver International Airport
