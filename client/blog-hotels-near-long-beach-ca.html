Hotels Near Long Beach, CA: Your Complete Guide

June 1, 2024 5 min read

Long Beach, a vibrant coastal city in Southern California, offers a diverse range of accommodations from luxury waterfront hotels to charming boutique properties. Whether you're visiting for the beach, the Queen Mary, or exploring the city's attractions, finding the right place to stay in Long Beach is essential for a great experience. This guide will help you navigate Long Beach's hotel scene.

Best Areas to Stay in Long Beach

Long Beach's neighborhoods each offer unique experiences:

  • Downtown: Urban center with luxury hotels
  • Shoreline Village: Waterfront area with resort hotels
  • Belmont Shore: Beach area with boutique hotels
  • Bixby Knolls: Upscale area with comfortable stays
  • Signal Hill: Hilltop area with panoramic views

Top Hotel Categories in Long Beach

Luxury Hotels

Experience the height of luxury at properties like The Hyatt Regency Long Beach, The Westin Long Beach, or The Renaissance Long Beach Hotel. These hotels offer world-class amenities, fine dining, and prime locations near major attractions.

Boutique Hotels

For a more intimate experience, consider boutique hotels in areas like Belmont Shore or Downtown. These properties often feature unique designs and personalized service.

Budget-Friendly Options

Long Beach offers several affordable hotel chains and independent properties that provide comfortable stays without breaking the bank. Look for deals in areas like North Long Beach or near the airport.

Tips for Booking Hotels in Long Beach

  • Book at least 2-3 months in advance for peak season
  • Consider major events like the Grand Prix
  • Look for hotels with included breakfast
  • Check for hotel packages that include attraction tickets
  • Read recent reviews to ensure the hotel meets your expectations

Best Time to Visit Long Beach

The best hotel rates are typically available during:

  • January and February (excluding holiday weekends)
  • Late summer (August)
  • Early December (before holiday season)

Transportation Tips

When choosing your hotel, consider:

  • Proximity to major attractions you plan to visit
  • Access to Long Beach Transit bus routes and ride-sharing services
  • Parking availability and costs
  • Distance from Long Beach Airport
